    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">字源</span>
      <span class="sh-py">zìyuán</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Etymology &amp; Structure</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="生">
      <div class="scholar-label">字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight</div>
      <p><strong>生 shēng</strong> is one of the oldest and most transparent characters in the writing system. The oracle-bone form shows a plant 屮 pushing upward through the ground 土 — a sprout emerging from the earth. Life, in its most primal pictographic form: the moment of <em>emergence.</em></p>
      <p>This single image radiates into four distinct semantic clusters that are all still active in modern Chinese: (1) <strong>life and living</strong> (生命, 生活, 生存); (2) <strong>birth and coming into being</strong> (出生, 诞生, 产生); (3) <strong>raw and unprocessed</strong> (生的, 生疏, 陌生 — things not yet cooked or cultivated); (4) <strong>student or junior person</strong> (学生, 先生 — one who is still "green," still growing).</p>
      <p>生 is also a productive radical: 性 (nature; gender), 星 (star), 姓 (surname), 胜 (victory), 剩 (leftover) all contain elements of 生's root meaning. Once you see the sprout, you see it everywhere.</p>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="life"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">生命</span>
      <span class="sh-py">shēngmìng</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Life — the Core Vocabulary</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">生命 · 生活 · 生存 — Three Registers of "Life"</span>
      <strong>生命</strong> shēngmìng = life as biological existence, the life-force itself (philosophical, medical)<br>
      <strong>生活</strong> shēnghuó = life as daily lived experience — "lifestyle," "livelihood"<br>
      <strong>生存</strong> shēngcún = survival — existence under pressure; the will to persist
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-n">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">生命</span>
          <span class="card-py">shēngmìng</span>
          <span class="card-en">life; the life-force</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">N 名词 míngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          生 shēng (life) + 命 mìng (fate; life-span; command). The weight of one's entire existence. More philosophical and solemn than 生活. 生命是宝贵的 "Life is precious." Also appears in science: 生命科学 life sciences.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">生命只有一次，要好好珍惜。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Shēngmìng zhǐ yǒu yī cì, yào hǎohǎo zhēnxī.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Life only comes once — treasure it well.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">生活</span>
          <span class="card-py">shēnghuó</span>
          <span class="card-en">life; to live; livelihood; lifestyle</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-vn">V/N 动名词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          生 shēng (live) + 活 huó (alive; to live). The most common word for everyday lived life. Works as noun (我的生活 "my life/lifestyle") and verb (我们在北京生活 "we live in Beijing"). 生活质量 quality of life · 生活方式 way of life.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我很喜欢这里的生活。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ hěn xǐhuān zhèlǐ de shēnghuó.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I really enjoy life here.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他们在农村生活了十年。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tāmen zài nóngcūn shēnghuó le shí nián.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">They lived in the countryside for ten years.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">生存</span>
          <span class="card-py">shēngcún</span>
          <span class="card-en">to survive; to subsist</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          生 shēng + 存 cún (to exist; to preserve). Survival — continuing to exist under difficult conditions. 生存竞争 "survival competition" · 生存危机 "survival crisis." A more pressured, primal register than 生活.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">在沙漠中生存需要很多技能。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zài shāmò zhōng shēngcún xūyào hěn duō jìnéng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Surviving in the desert requires many skills.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">出生</span>
          <span class="card-py">chūshēng</span>
          <span class="card-en">to be born; birth</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          出 chū (to exit; to come out) + 生 shēng (life; birth). The emergence from the womb. Used for birthdays (出生日期 date of birth) and biographical facts (他出生在北京 "he was born in Beijing").
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">她出生在1990年。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā chūshēng zài yījiǔjiǔlíng nián.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">She was born in 1990.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">产生</span>
          <span class="card-py">chǎnshēng</span>
          <span class="card-en">to produce; to give rise to; to emerge</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          产 chǎn (to produce; output) + 生 shēng (arise). For abstract and concrete creation — ideas, problems, emotions, consequences arising. 产生影响 "to have an effect" · 产生误会 "to give rise to a misunderstanding."
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这个决定产生了很大的影响。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhège juédìng chǎnshēng le hěn dà de yǐngxiǎng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">This decision produced a large impact.</div>
          </div>
        </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-n">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">学生</span>
          <span class="card-py">xuésheng</span>
          <span class="card-en">student</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">N 名词 míngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          学 xué (to study; to learn) + 生 shēng (one who is still "green"; a junior person). The student as someone still growing, not yet ripe. One of the most common nouns in Chinese — every school context relies on it.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我是大学生。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ shì dàxuéshēng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I am a university student.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-n">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">先生</span>
          <span class="card-py">xiānsheng</span>
          <span class="card-en">Mr.; teacher; husband; gentleman</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">N 名词 míngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          先 xiān (first; ahead; earlier) + 生 shēng (one born; person). "One born earlier" — originally a teacher or Confucian master; now the standard polite term for "Mr." and also "husband" in formal contexts (我先生 "my husband"). A word whose meaning has drifted beautifully from teacher → gentleman → Mr.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">王先生，请进。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wáng xiānsheng, qǐng jìn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Mr. Wang, please come in.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我先生在银行工作。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ xiānsheng zài yínháng gōngzuò.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">My husband works at a bank.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-n">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">医生</span>
          <span class="card-py">yīshēng</span>
          <span class="card-en">doctor; physician</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">N 名词 míngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          医 yī (medicine; to heal) + 生 shēng (practitioner). The standard word for doctor in mainland China. Note the 生 suffix — it originally meant "one who practices/studies" (compare 学生, 先生). 看医生 = "to see a doctor."
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">你应该去看医生。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Nǐ yīnggāi qù kàn yīshēng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">You should go see a doctor.</div>
          </div>
        </div>

        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">生</span>

        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-adj">Adj 形容词 xíngróngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          Used standalone or as a prefix: 生鱼片 raw fish (sashimi) · 生肉 raw meat · 生米 uncooked rice. The "not yet transformed" meaning extends to people and situations: 生人 stranger · 生词 new vocabulary word (one you haven't "cooked" yet into your knowledge).
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这肉还是生的，要再煮一会儿。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhè ròu háishi shēng de, yào zài zhǔ yīhuìr.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">This meat is still raw — cook it a little longer.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-adj">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">陌生</span>
          <span class="card-py">mòshēng</span>
          <span class="card-en">unfamiliar; strange; unknown</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-adj">Adj 形容词 xíngróngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          陌 mò (a stranger; a path between rice fields — where you encounter people you don't know) + 生 shēng (unprocessed; unfamiliar). For unfamiliar places, strangers, and new experiences. 陌生人 = stranger. 对我来说很陌生 = "It's unfamiliar to me."
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">到了新城市，一切都感到陌生。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Dào le xīn chéngshì, yīqiè dōu gǎndào mòshēng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Arriving in a new city, everything felt unfamiliar.</div>
          </div>

      <div class="scholar-label">记忆法 jìyìfǎ · Master Retention Image</div>
      <p>Visualize a green sprout pushing up through dark soil — that is 生, and that image unlocks everything. Life is the sprout (生命). Daily life is the plant growing day by day (生活). Raw food is the plant not yet cooked (生肉). A student is a tender green shoot not yet mature (学生). A stranger is a plant you've never seen before in your garden (陌生人). Everything 生 means is one stage or another of that first emergence from the earth.</p>
