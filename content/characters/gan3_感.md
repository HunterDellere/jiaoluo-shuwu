    <div class="section-head">

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="感">
      <div class="scholar-label">字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight</div>
      <p><strong>感 gǎn = 咸 xián</strong> (all; universal; reaching everywhere) <strong>+ 心 xīn</strong> (heart). The oracle-bone form of 咸 depicts a battle-axe striking a mouth — a sound that reaches all ears. Placed above 心, the compound becomes: <em>that which the heart universally resonates with.</em> This is why 感 covers such vast emotional terrain: sensation, gratitude, inspiration, contagion — all are versions of the heart being struck and vibrating.</p>
      <p>In Japanese, the same character is read <em>kan</em> — 感じる <em>kanjiru</em> "to feel," 感謝 <em>kansha</em> "gratitude," 感動 <em>kandō</em> "being moved." The semantic field maps with near-perfect fidelity, making 感 one of the most powerful Sino-Japanese anchors for learners of both languages.</p>
      <p>The cosmological logic runs deeper still: in classical Chinese thought, the universe operates through <strong>感应 gǎnyìng</strong> — sympathetic resonance. Heaven <em>feels</em> human affairs and responds; humans <em>feel</em> Heaven's movements. Every 感-word is a different shape that resonance takes.</p>
    </div>

    <!-- ── PERCEPTION ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="perception"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">感知</span>
      <span class="sh-py">gǎnzhī</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Core Perception Verbs</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <!-- 感到 -->
      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">感到</span>
          <span class="card-py">gǎn dào</span>
          <span class="card-en">to feel; to register a state</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          感 gǎn (feel) + 到 dào (arrive at · result marker). The 到 is a <strong>resultative complement</strong> — it signals the feeling has <em>landed</em>, been registered as a completed state. The most neutral, all-purpose feeling verb. Almost always takes an adjectival or clausal complement.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我感到很疲惫。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ gǎndào hěn píbèi.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I feel very exhausted.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他感到一阵寒意。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā gǎndào yī zhèn hányì.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He felt a sudden chill.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我感到一切都变了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ gǎndào yīqiè dōu biàn le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I feel that everything has changed.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">语法 yǔfǎ · Grammar</span>
          Pattern: <code>感到 + adj / noun phrase / clause</code>. Examples: 感到骄傲 proud · 感到难受 unwell · 感到惊讶 surprised. Register: neutral — works in speech and writing equally. More <em>objective</em> in flavor than 感觉.
        </div>
      </div>

      <!-- 感觉 -->
      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">感觉</span>
          <span class="card-py">gǎnjué</span>
          <span class="card-en">feeling; impression; to feel (V &amp; N)</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-vn">V/N 动名词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          感 gǎn + 觉 jué (to awaken; become aware — originally: waking from sleep, the first moment of consciousness). <strong>Dual-category word</strong> — functions as both verb and noun. The most flexible word in the 感 family. Covers gut feeling, subjective impression, intuition.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这种感觉很奇妙。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhè zhǒng gǎnjué hěn qímiào.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">This feeling is wonderful. <em>[noun use]</em></div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我感觉他不太高兴。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ gǎnjué tā bù tài gāoxìng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I get the sense he's not very happy. <em>[verb use]</em></div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">感觉有点不对劲。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Gǎnjué yǒudiǎn bú duìjìn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Something feels off. <em>[colloquial drop-subject]</em></div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">辨析 biànxī · Comparison of the Four</span>
          <strong>感觉</strong> = subjective impression, includes gut feeling. <strong>感到</strong> = registering a more objective state (resultative). <strong>感受</strong> gǎnshòu (feel + 受 receive) = deeper, embodied, reflective experience — art, travel, introspection. <strong>感知</strong> gǎnzhī (feel + 知 know) = perceptual cognition, academic register.
        </div>
      </div>

      <!-- 感受 -->
      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">感受</span>
          <span class="card-py">gǎnshòu</span>
          <span class="card-en">lived experience; to experience deeply</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-vn">V/N 动名词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          感 gǎn + 受 shòu (to receive; to undergo; to be a recipient). Something comes <em>to you</em> and is taken in. Emphasizes the experiential <em>process</em>, not just the end-state — richer and more embodied than 感觉. Common in art criticism, travel writing, and introspective prose.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">你有什么感受？</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Nǐ yǒu shénme gǎnshòu?</div>
            <div class="ex-en">What is your experience of this? What do you feel?</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我想感受大自然的美丽。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ xiǎng gǎnshòu dàzìrán de měilì.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I want to experience the beauty of nature.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <!-- 感知 -->
      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">感知</span>
          <span class="card-py">gǎnzhī</span>
          <span class="card-en">perceptual cognition; to perceive</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-vn">V/N 动名词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          感 gǎn + 知 zhī (to know; cognition). Perception as an act of knowing — the full cycle from sensation to understanding. The most formal and academic of the four.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">感知觉是心理学的基础概念。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Gǎnzhījué shì xīnlǐxué de jīchǔ gàiniàn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Perception and awareness are foundational concepts in psychology.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">天人感应是中国古代哲学的核心概念。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tiān rén gǎnyìng shì Zhōngguó gǔdài zhéxué de héxīn gàiniàn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Heaven-human resonance is a core concept of ancient Chinese philosophy.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">语域 yǔyù · Register</span>
          Academic and formal. Appears in psychology (感知觉 gǎnzhījué), cognitive science, and classical philosophy. In ordinary speech, 感觉 covers this ground.
        </div>
      </div>

    </div><!-- /cards perception -->

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">谢谢 → 感谢 → 感激 → 感恩 · The Gratitude Gradient</span>
      <strong>谢谢</strong> xièxie (casual, spoken) → <strong>感谢</strong> gǎnxiè (sincere, formal) → <strong>感激</strong> gǎnjī (deep, emotionally indebted) → <strong>感恩</strong> gǎn'ēn (Confucian virtue — honoring all grace received)

    <div class="cards">

      <!-- 感谢 -->
      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">感谢</span>
          <span class="card-py">gǎnxiè</span>
          <span class="card-en">to thank sincerely; gratitude (formal)</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-vn">V/N 动名词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          感 gǎn + 谢 xiè (to thank; originally: water overflowing — <em>pouring out</em> one's feelings). Warmer and more sincere than 谢谢. Standard in writing, speeches, and formal situations. Also functions as a noun in 表示感谢.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">感谢您的帮助！</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Gǎnxiè nín de bāngzhù!</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Thank you sincerely for your help!</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">感谢你来。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Gǎnxiè nǐ lái.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Thank you for coming.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">请允许我表示感谢。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Qǐng yǔnxǔ wǒ biǎoshì gǎnxiè.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Please allow me to express my gratitude. <em>[noun use]</em></div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">语法 yǔfǎ · Grammar</span>
          Pattern: <code>感谢 + person + verb phrase</code>: 感谢你来 "thank you for coming." As noun: 表示感谢 biǎoshì gǎnxiè "to express gratitude."
        </div>
      </div>

      <!-- 感激 -->
      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">感激</span>
          <span class="card-py">gǎnjī</span>
          <span class="card-en">deeply grateful; moved by another's kindness</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          感 gǎn + 激 jī (to stir; to agitate; rapids — turbulent water). The heart <em>stirred up</em> by a significant kindness. Implies emotional debt that moves you. Stronger than 感谢; use for meaningful help, not everyday thanks.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我非常感激你在我最难的时候帮助我。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ fēicháng gǎnjī nǐ zài wǒ zuì nán de shíhòu bāngzhù wǒ.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I'm deeply grateful you helped me in my hardest time.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">成语 chéngyǔ · Idiom</span>
          <strong>感激涕零</strong> gǎnjī tì líng — "gratitude so deep that tears fall." 涕零 = tears streaming down the face. Classical idiom for overwhelming thankfulness.
        </div>
      </div>

      <!-- 感恩 -->
      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">感恩</span>
          <span class="card-py">gǎn'ēn</span>
          <span class="card-en">to honor all grace received; gratitude as virtue</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          感 gǎn + 恩 ēn (grace; benevolence — the kindness of parents, teachers, heaven). A <strong>Confucian virtue</strong>, not merely an emotion. Recognizing and honoring all goodness received throughout one's life. The deepest gratitude word.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我们应该感恩父母的养育之恩。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒmen yīnggāi gǎn'ēn fùmǔ de yǎngyù zhī ēn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">We should be grateful for the grace of our parents' upbringing.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">文化 wénhuà · Culture</span>
          感恩节 Gǎn'ēnjié = Thanksgiving. The character 恩 ēn appears in: 恩人 ēnrén benefactor · 恩情 ēnqíng kindness-bond · 忘恩负义 wàng'ēn fùyì "ungrateful" (lit. forget-grace-betray-duty).
        </div>
      </div>

      <!-- 感动 -->
      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">感动</span>
          <span class="card-py">gǎndòng</span>
          <span class="card-en">to be moved; to touch someone's heart</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          感 gǎn + 动 dòng (to move). The heart <em>set in motion</em> by something beautiful, kind, or touching. One of the most emotionally central verbs in Chinese — high frequency in literature, film, and daily speech.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这部电影让我非常感动。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhè bù diànyǐng ràng wǒ fēicháng gǎndòng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">This film moved me deeply.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他的故事感动了所有人。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā de gùshì gǎndòng le suǒyǒu rén.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">His story moved everyone.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">语法 yǔfǎ · Grammar</span>
          Intransitive: 我感动了. Transitive: 他感动了我. Causative 让/使 pattern very common. Related adjective: <strong>感人</strong> gǎnrén (touching, moving — used attributively: 感人的故事 a touching story).
        </div>
      </div>

      <!-- 感慨 -->
      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">感慨</span>
          <span class="card-py">gǎnkǎi</span>
          <span class="card-en">bittersweet swell of emotion; to sigh with feeling</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-vn">V/N 动名词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          感 gǎn + 慨 kǎi (to sigh; to lament — a person exhaling with grief). The bittersweet wave felt when contemplating time, change, and transience. Not sadness, not joy — the specific ache of <em>things passing.</em> Quintessentially literary. Common in memoirs, essays, and poetry.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">回到故乡，他不禁感慨万千。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Huí dào gùxiāng, tā bùjīn gǎnkǎi wànqiān.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Returning to his hometown, he was overwhelmed by a thousand feelings.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">成语 chéngyǔ</span>
          <strong>感慨万千</strong> gǎnkǎi wànqiān — "ten thousand emotions surging." The go-to phrase for homecomings, reunions, and witnessing great change.
        </div>
      </div>

      <!-- 感叹 -->
      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">感叹</span>
          <span class="card-py">gǎntàn</span>
          <span class="card-en">to exclaim with feeling; to marvel</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          感 gǎn + 叹 tàn (to sigh; to exclaim). When emotion erupts as an exclamation. <strong>Memorable fact:</strong> 感叹号 gǎntànhào = the exclamation mark (!). The character literally encodes the grammar of feeling.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他不禁感叹时光飞逝。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā bùjīn gǎntàn shíguāng fēishì.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He couldn't help but marvel at how quickly time flies.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <!-- 感伤 -->
      <div class="card c-adj">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">感伤</span>
          <span class="card-py">gǎnshāng</span>
          <span class="card-en">melancholy; tender sadness</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-adj">Adj 形容词 xíngróngcí</span></div>

      <div class="scholar-label">记忆法 jìyìfǎ · Master Retention Image</div>
      <p>Imagine 感 gǎn as a <strong>tuning fork pressed against the heart.</strong> When something real strikes it — a kindness, a piece of music, a pathogen, a homecoming — the fork vibrates and the heart resonates. The upper element 咸 (universal, reaching all) tells you the vibration can travel anywhere: upward into gratitude 感恩, outward into others 感染, inward into insight 感悟, dissolving into bittersweet sighs 感慨. Every 感-word is a different shape that resonance takes.</p>
