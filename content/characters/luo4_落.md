    <!-- ── ETYMOLOGY ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="etymology"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">字源</span>
      <span class="sh-py">zìyuán</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Etymology &amp; Structure</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="落">
      <div class="scholar-label">字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight</div>
      <p><strong>落 = 艹</strong> (grass radical) <strong>+ 洛 luò</strong> (phonetic — a river name). The original meaning was leaves falling from plants — the grass radical anchors it to the botanical world. Water falling was already embedded in the phonetic 洛 (a river whose name evokes flowing and settling). Together: the natural downward motion of organic things arriving at rest.</p>
      <p>This character is the second half of 角落 jiǎoluò — the corner where things settle, the nook that gives this site its name. 落 in that compound contributes not motion but <em>arrival</em> — the state of having come to rest in a particular place. A book-room in the corner is books that have landed there.</p>
      <p>落 also appears as a phonetic-semantic pivot across many characters: 骆 luò (camel — the animal of caravans that settle at oases), 洛 Luò (the Luo River, historically crucial), 络 luò (a net; to entwine — things falling and wrapping together).</p>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">落下</span>
          <span class="card-py">luòxià / làxia</span>
          <span class="card-en">to fall; to drop behind; to be left behind</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          Two distinct pronunciations with different meanings. <strong>luò xià</strong> = to fall downward (rain falling, price falling). <strong>là·xia</strong> (neutral tone) = to fall behind, to leave something behind by accident: 把钥匙落下了 "left the keys behind."
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">树叶落下来了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Shùyè luò xiàlái le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">The leaves are falling down.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他把钱包落在出租车上了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā bǎ qiánbāo là zài chūzūchē shàng le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He left his wallet in the taxi.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">发音辨析 · Pronunciation Split</span>
          The là reading (neutral second syllable) is colloquial North Chinese; in the South and in formal registers luò is used throughout. This alternation is one of the more notable tone-register splits in Mandarin.
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">降落</span>
          <span class="card-py">jiànglùo</span>
          <span class="card-en">to land; to descend (aircraft); to come down</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          降 jiàng (to descend, to lower) + 落 luò (to land). The standard word for an aircraft landing. Also used for anything descending to earth: 降落伞 jiànglùosǎn = parachute.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">飞机安全降落了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Fēijī ānquán jiànglùo le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">The plane landed safely.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">落后</span>
          <span class="card-py">luòhòu</span>
          <span class="card-en">to fall behind; backward; underdeveloped</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          落 luò + 后 hòu (behind). To lag behind others in a race, in development, or in progress. Used in sports, economics, and social commentary. Carries a normative weight — something that has fallen behind should catch up.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我们不能落后于时代。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒmen bù néng luòhòu yú shídài.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">We cannot fall behind the times.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">跌落</span>
          <span class="card-py">diēluò</span>
          <span class="card-en">to plummet; to tumble down sharply</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          跌 diē (to stumble, to fall sharply) + 落 luò. A more violent or dramatic fall than 落 alone — prices plummeting, a person tumbling. Common in financial and news contexts.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">股价昨日跌落百分之十。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Gǔjià zuórì diēluò bǎifēnzhī shí.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Stock prices plummeted ten percent yesterday.</div>
          </div>

    <!-- ── 落 SUFFIX ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="suffix"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">X + 落</span>
      <span class="sh-py">X + luò</span>
      <span class="sh-en">落 as Suffix — Villages, Nooks &amp; Settled Places</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="落">
      <div class="scholar-label">词缀洞见 cízhuì dòngjiàn · Suffix Insight</div>
      <p>When 落 follows another element, it often means <em>a settled place, a cluster of things that have landed together.</em> This pattern is especially visible in place names and spatial vocabulary. A 部落 bùluò (tribe, clan) is literally a group that has settled together. A 村落 cūnluò (village) is a place where people have set down roots. An 角落 jiǎoluò (corner) is where things settle at the edge.</p>
      <p>The same logic appears in less obvious words: 零落 línglùo (scattered, withered) describes leaves that have fallen and lie scattered; 沦落 lúnluò (to sink into degradation) is a person who has fallen to a lower place. All are variations on the image of something descending and arriving at a new — often diminished — state.</p>
    </div>

        <span class="cy-en">to throw stones at someone who has fallen in a well — to kick someone when they're down</span>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">落花流水</span>
        <span class="cy-py">luò huā liú shuǐ</span>
        <span class="cy-en">fallen blossoms and flowing water — spring's end; utter defeat</span>
        <span class="cy-note">Lit: falling-flowers flowing-water. A classical image from Tang and Song poetry for the beauty of spring's end. Also used for utter defeat: 打得落花流水 "beaten to pieces."</span>
      </div>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">大起大落</span>
        <span class="cy-py">dà qǐ dà luò</span>
        <span class="cy-en">dramatic rises and falls — extreme volatility and turbulence</span>
        <span class="cy-note">Lit: great-rise great-fall. For lives, markets, or dynasties marked by dramatic swings — the roller-coaster image. Common in financial commentary and biographical writing.</span>

        <span class="cy-py">guā shú dì luò</span>
