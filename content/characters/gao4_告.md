    <span class="section-anchor" id="etymology"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">字源</span><span class="sh-py">zìyuán</span><span class="sh-en">Etymology — Ox-Head Over Mouth</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="告">
      <div class="scholar-label">字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight</div>
      <p>The oracle-bone form of <strong>告</strong> shows an ox head (<strong>牛 niú</strong>) above a mouth (<strong>口 kǒu</strong>). The classical interpretation: when a man presents an ox as a ritual offering or gift to a superior — a lord, an ancestor, the spirits — he simultaneously speaks the reason for the offering. The speaking that accompanies a solemn presentation is 告: to announce, to declare, to make known to a person of higher standing.</p>
      <p>This origin explains why 告 carries a strong sense of directionality — the communication flows upward or outward with authority. You 告 a superior, an official, a court, or the public. It is not casual speech but announced speech: a report filed, a charge leveled, a warning issued, an advertisement placed.</p>
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="core"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">核心义</span><span class="sh-py">héxīn yì</span><span class="sh-en">Core Meanings — Tell, Announce, Accuse</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="告">
      <div class="scholar-label">义场 yìchǎng · Meaning Field</div>
      <p><strong>To tell / inform (告诉).</strong> The everyday sense: 告诉我 (tell me), 告诉他你的想法 (tell him what you think). 告诉 gàosu is among the first verbs learners encounter.</p>
      <p><strong>To announce / notify (通告, 公告).</strong> Official, public-facing declarations: 公告 (public announcement), 通告 (circular notice), 布告 (posted notice). The register is formal — these appear on walls, in government documents, on websites.</p>
      <p><strong>To accuse / sue (控告, 告状).</strong> The legal register traces directly to the ritual origin: presenting a grievance to a superior for judgment. 告状 literally means "to file a complaint-shape" — to bring a case.</p>
      <p><strong>To warn (警告).</strong> 警 (vigilance) + 告 (announce) = to announce vigilance. Used for everything from traffic signs to diplomatic warnings.</p>
      <p><strong>To bid farewell (告别).</strong> 告别 gào bié — to formally announce a parting. The formality of 告 makes this more ceremonial than 再见.</p>
    </div>
