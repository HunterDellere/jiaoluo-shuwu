    <span class="section-anchor" id="etymology"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">字源</span>
      <span class="sh-py">zìyuán</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Etymology &amp; Structure</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="命">
      <div class="scholar-label">字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight</div>
      <p><strong>命 mìng = 令 (lìng, order) enclosing 口 (kǒu, mouth)</strong>. An ideographic compound. 令 itself shows a kneeling figure receiving a directive from above, and 命 inserts a mouth into that frame: the voice that speaks the order. The structure says: <em>命 is an order issued by a mouth.</em></p>
      <p>命 begins as a verb meaning <strong>to issue a command</strong>. From there, three meanings unfold along a single line of force, each one a step further from the speaker.</p>
      <p><strong>1. The command itself.</strong> 命令 mìnglìng (an order, a directive). The original sense, still living in modern Chinese.<br>
      <strong>2. The decree of Heaven.</strong> What the cosmos has commanded for you. 天命 tiānmìng (the Mandate of Heaven; the destiny appointed by Heaven). The Confucian classics carry this sense as the foundation of legitimate rule and the source of personal vocation.<br>
      <strong>3. Life as the carrying-out of that decree.</strong> 生命 shēngmìng (life), 寿命 shòumìng (life-span). What you have been ordered to live; the time you have been given.</p>
      <p>Three senses, one logic. A 命 is something handed down: an order from a superior, an order from Heaven, the order to be alive. Hence the chilling <strong>救命 jiùmìng</strong>, "save my life," literally "rescue my command-to-live." Hence <strong>没命 méimìng</strong>, "to lose one's life," literally "to no longer have a command." Life in this character is not a possession. It is something you carry on someone else's instruction, and it can be revoked.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">构词规律 gòucí guīlǜ · Three Lines of Force</span>
      <strong>命 as life</strong> → 生命 shēngmìng (life), 寿命 shòumìng (life-span), 救命 jiùmìng (save a life), 拼命 pīnmìng (risk one's life).<br>
      <strong>命 as fate</strong> → 命运 mìngyùn (fate), 算命 suànmìng (fortune-telling), 命相 mìngxiàng (fate-physiognomy), 天命 tiānmìng (Mandate of Heaven).<br>
      <strong>命 as order</strong> → 命令 mìnglìng (a command), 任命 rènmìng (to appoint), 革命 gémìng (revolution; "stripping the mandate"), 听命 tīngmìng (to obey).

    <!-- ── LIFE ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="life"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">生命</span>
      <span class="sh-py">shēngmìng</span>
      <span class="sh-en">The Life-Cluster · 命 as the Time Given</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-n">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">生命</span>
          <span class="card-py">shēngmìng</span>
          <span class="card-en">life (the abstract concept; the fact of being alive)</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">N 名词 míngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          生 shēng (to be born; to live) plus 命. The standard modern word for <em>life</em> in the broad sense: biological life, the human life-course, life as a value worth protecting. Distinct from 生活 shēnghuó (daily life, way of living): 生命 is the precious thing you have once, 生活 is what you do with it. 生命科学 "life sciences," 生命力 "vitality, life-force," 珍惜生命 "to cherish life."
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">生命是宝贵的。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Shēngmìng shì bǎoguì de.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Life is precious.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他用自己的生命救了那个孩子。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā yòng zìjǐ de shēngmìng jiù le nàge háizi.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He used his own life to save that child.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-n">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">寿命</span>
          <span class="card-py">shòumìng</span>
          <span class="card-en">life-span; lifetime; (of a thing) durability, service life</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">N 名词 míngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          寿 shòu (longevity; long life) plus 命. The measured length of a life. Used both for living beings (人均寿命 "average human life-span") and, by extension, for objects (机器的寿命 "the service life of a machine"). The pairing makes the metaphysics explicit: a 寿命 is the duration of a 命, the span the command-to-live runs before it is rescinded.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">中国人的平均寿命越来越长。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhōngguó rén de píngjūn shòumìng yuè lái yuè cháng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">The average life-span of Chinese people is getting longer and longer.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">救命</span>
          <span class="card-py">jiùmìng</span>
          <span class="card-en">to save a life; (as exclamation) Help! Save me!</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          救 jiù (to rescue) plus 命. The standard cry for help in distress, what you shout in a fire, drowning, or attack. Literally "rescue [my] life." 救命恩人 "the benefactor who saved one's life" is a heavy phrase: in Chinese culture, the one who saves your life is owed a debt that does not fully clear in this lifetime.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">救命啊！有人掉到水里了！</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Jiùmìng a! Yǒu rén diào dào shuǐ lǐ le!</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Help! Someone fell into the water!</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他是我的救命恩人。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā shì wǒ de jiùmìng ēnrén.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He is the benefactor who saved my life.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">拼命</span>
          <span class="card-py">pīnmìng</span>
          <span class="card-en">to risk one's life; to do something with everything one has</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          拼 pīn (to throw together; to stake) plus 命. To "stake one's life" on something, used both literally (combat, rescue) and figuratively for any effort given without holding back. 拼命工作 "to work like one's life depends on it" is the standard phrase for sustained, all-in effort. The character 命 is what makes the phrase feel heavier than English "to do all-out": the Chinese phrase says, <em>I have put my command-to-live on the table.</em>
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他拼命工作，就是为了孩子能上好学校。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā pīnmìng gōngzuò, jiùshì wèile háizi néng shàng hǎo xuéxiào.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He works himself to the bone, all so his child can attend a good school.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div><!-- /cards life -->

    <!-- ── FATE ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="fate"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">命运</span>
      <span class="sh-py">mìngyùn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">The Fate-Cluster · 命 as What Has Been Decreed</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-n">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">命运</span>
          <span class="card-py">mìngyùn</span>
          <span class="card-en">fate; destiny; the trajectory of one's life</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">N 名词 míngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          命 plus 运 yùn (movement; turning; fortune). The compound joins what is fixed (命, the decree) and what is in motion (运, the turning of the wheel). The distinction is alive in colloquial use: someone may have a good 命 but bad 运, a strong underlying allotment whose current run-out is unlucky. 改变命运 "to change one's fate" is the modern aspirational phrase; the very fact that the verb 改变 can take 命运 as object signals the modern departure from the older fatalism.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">教育可以改变一个人的命运。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Jiàoyù kěyǐ gǎibiàn yī ge rén de mìngyùn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Education can change a person's fate.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我们要掌握自己的命运。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒmen yào zhǎngwò zìjǐ de mìngyùn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">We must take hold of our own fate.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">算命</span>
          <span class="card-py">suànmìng</span>
          <span class="card-en">to tell fortunes; to calculate one's fate</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-vn">V 动词 / N 名词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          算 suàn (to calculate) plus 命. The phrase reveals the cultural assumption: a 命 is something that can be <em>computed</em>. Given the year, month, day, and hour of birth (生辰八字 shēngchén bāzì, "the eight characters of birth"), a fortune-teller can read out the trajectory. 算命先生 "a fortune-teller." Although officially discouraged in mainland China, the practice remains widespread, especially around marriage matching, naming children, and choosing auspicious dates.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">她去找一位算命先生看自己的婚姻。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā qù zhǎo yī wèi suànmìng xiānsheng kàn zìjǐ de hūnyīn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">She went to a fortune-teller to look into her own marriage.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-n">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">天命</span>
          <span class="card-py">tiānmìng</span>
          <span class="card-en">the Mandate of Heaven; the decree of Heaven; one's heavenly vocation</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">N 名词 míngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          天 tiān (Heaven) plus 命. The political-philosophical concept central to classical Chinese thought: a ruler's authority is conferred by Heaven, and Heaven withdraws it from the unrighteous, the doctrine that legitimated dynastic change for over two thousand years. Confucius's autobiographical 五十而知天命 "at fifty I knew the Mandate of Heaven" gives the phrase its personal sense too: knowing what one was placed in this life to do.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">五十而知天命。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǔshí ér zhī tiānmìng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">At fifty, I knew the Mandate of Heaven. <em>(Confucius, Analects 2.4)</em></div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div><!-- /cards fate -->

    <span class="section-anchor" id="command"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">命令</span>
      <span class="sh-py">mìnglìng</span>
      <span class="sh-en">The Order-Cluster · 命 as Authority Speaking</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">命令</span>
          <span class="card-py">mìnglìng</span>
          <span class="card-en">to order; an order, a directive</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-vn">V 动词 / N 名词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          命 plus 令 lìng (order). A synonym-compound: both characters mean "command," doubled for clarity. Used for formal, top-down orders in military, governmental, and parental contexts. Cooler in tone than 让 ràng (to let, to make) and harder than 要求 yāoqiú (to request). 服从命令 "to obey orders" is the formula in disciplined contexts.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">士兵必须服从命令。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Shìbīng bìxū fúcóng mìnglìng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Soldiers must obey orders.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">任命</span>
          <span class="card-py">rènmìng</span>
          <span class="card-en">to appoint (to a post)</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          任 rèn (to entrust; office, post) plus 命. To formally place someone in a role by official decree. The standard verb in government, corporate, and academic contexts for the act of appointment. Distinguished from 选 (to elect) and 聘 (to hire by contract): 任命 carries the weight of being chosen and ordered into a position, not selected by vote or hired by agreement.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他被任命为新任校长。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā bèi rènmìng wéi xīn rèn xiàozhǎng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He was appointed as the new principal.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-n">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">革命</span>
          <span class="card-py">gémìng</span>
          <span class="card-en">revolution (literally "to strip away the mandate")</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-vn">V 动词 / N 名词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          革 gé (leather; to strip; to change) plus 命. Originally classical: to remove the Mandate of Heaven (天命) from a dynasty that had lost it, the pre-modern theory of legitimate dynastic overthrow. In the late nineteenth century the term was used to translate the Western political concept "revolution" and acquired its modern political weight. 文化大革命 (Cultural Revolution) and 辛亥革命 (Xinhai Revolution) are the two reference points.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">辛亥革命结束了中国两千多年的帝制。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Xīnhài Gémìng jiéshù le Zhōngguó liǎng qiān duō nián de dìzhì.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">The Xinhai Revolution ended over two thousand years of imperial rule in China.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div><!-- /cards command -->

    <span class="section-anchor" id="chengyu"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">成语</span>
      <span class="sh-py">chéngyǔ</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Idioms &amp; Set Phrases</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="chengyu-grid">

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">命中注定</span>
        <span class="cy-py">mìng zhōng zhù dìng</span>
        <span class="cy-en">fated; predestined; "written into one's life from the start"</span>
        <span class="cy-note">命中 "in the [allotment of one's] life" plus 注定 "fixed; settled; recorded." Used both seriously (a tragic outcome could not have been avoided) and lightly (joking that two friends were destined to meet). Common in romantic discourse: 我们是命中注定的 "we were meant to be." The phrase encodes the older fatalist worldview into ordinary speech.</span>
      </div>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">听天由命</span>
        <span class="cy-py">tīng tiān yóu mìng</span>
        <span class="cy-en">to listen to Heaven and yield to fate; to resign oneself to whatever happens</span>
        <span class="cy-note">听天 "listen to Heaven" plus 由命 "let fate take its course." The classical formula for resignation, used both admiringly (Stoic equanimity) and critically (passive surrender). Often follows a phrase indicating that one has done all one can: 尽人事，听天命 "do all that is humanly possible, then yield to Heaven's command."</span>
      </div>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">相依为命</span>
        <span class="cy-py">xiāng yī wéi mìng</span>
        <span class="cy-en">to depend on one another for life; to be each other's only support</span>
        <span class="cy-note">相依 "mutually depending" plus 为命 "for life." Said of two people whose survival depends on each other: a widow and her child, two siblings orphaned together, an old couple in their final years. Carries deep emotional weight; not used lightly. Often appears in family stories and biographical writing.</span>
      </div>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">舍生忘死</span>
        <span class="cy-py">shě shēng wàng sǐ</span>
        <span class="cy-en">to give up one's life and forget death; to face danger without thought of self</span>
        <span class="cy-note">A standard high-register chengyu for selfless courage, used for soldiers, rescue workers, doctors during epidemics. The phrase doesn't contain 命 directly but belongs to its semantic field: it names the willingness to set down the command-to-live for something larger.</span>
      </div>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">人命关天</span>
        <span class="cy-py">rén mìng guān tiān</span>
        <span class="cy-en">a human life is a matter of Heaven; "lives are at stake," used to underscore extreme gravity</span>
        <span class="cy-note">人命 "human life" plus 关天 "concerns Heaven." The phrase invoked when human lives are on the line in medical, legal, or rescue contexts, to communicate that ordinary calculations no longer apply. The implicit logic: a life belongs to Heaven; touching it is a cosmic, not merely human, matter.</span>
      </div>

    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="命" style="margin-top:2.5rem;">
      <div class="scholar-label">记忆法 jìyìfǎ · Master Retention Image</div>
      <p>See the structure: 令 (a kneeling figure receiving an order from above) with 口 (a mouth) tucked inside. A directive, given out loud, kneeling to receive it. That image is the whole character. The order is what 命 fundamentally <em>is</em>; everything else (fate, life-span, the cry "save me") is what happens once the order has been issued.</p>
      <p>Hold the three meanings on one line: <strong>命令</strong> (the order is given), then <strong>天命</strong> (the order comes from Heaven), then <strong>生命</strong> (your life is the carrying-out of that order). When the cry 救命 is shouted, it asks not "save my body" but "rescue the order I have been given to live." That is why the word lands so heavily in Chinese: every time you hear it, the whole hierarchy is in the word.</p>
