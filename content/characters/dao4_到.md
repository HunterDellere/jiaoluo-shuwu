    <!-- ── ETYMOLOGY ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="etymology"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">字源</span>
      <span class="sh-py">zìyuán</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Etymology &amp; Structure</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="到">
      <div class="scholar-label">字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight</div>
      <p><strong>到 dào = 至 zhì</strong> (to arrive — the semantic component) <strong>+ 刀 dāo</strong> (knife — the phonetic component). 至 zhì is the older word for arrival, and it carries the full weight of the meaning: oracle bone inscriptions show an arrow that has reached its target and struck the ground, the downward stroke puncturing a horizontal surface. The arrow does not hover; it lands. That moment of contact, of the projectile finding its mark, is 至 — and by inheritance, 到.</p>
      <p>刀 contributes the dào sound and little else semantically. But the visual pairing is memorable: the arrival image on the left (至, arrow reaching ground), the knife on the right (刀). Some scholars see a martial resonance — a blade reaching its target — though the phonetic explanation is sufficient. The character has been stable since the Warring States period.</p>
      <p>至 zhì survives in literary Chinese as a more formal and classical word for arrival: 至今 (up to now), 至于 (as for; to the extent of), 冬至 (winter solstice, "when winter arrives"). 到 displaced it in spoken Chinese and became the everyday word for arrival — and eventually the most productive resultative complement in the language.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">构形 gòuxíng · Character Structure</span>
      <strong>至 zhì</strong> (semantic: to arrive, arrow hitting ground) + <strong>刀 dāo</strong> (phonetic: provides the dào sound)<br>
      至 itself: oracle bone shows an arrow ↓ above a horizontal ground line — the moment of landing.<br>
      到 inherits 至's arrival semantics wholesale; 刀 is purely phonetic reinforcement.<br>
      Compare: 致 zhì (to cause, to convey — 至 + 攴), 室 shì (room — 至 + 宀, the place one arrives at).
    </div>

    <!-- ── 到 AS MAIN VERB ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="mainverb"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">主动词</span>
      <span class="sh-py">zhǔ dòngcí</span>
      <span class="sh-en">到 as Main Verb — Arrive, Reach, Accomplish</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="到">
      <div class="scholar-label">语法洞见 yǔfǎ dòngjiàn · Grammar Insight</div>
      <p>As a standalone main verb, 到 expresses physical or abstract arrival at a destination. The destination follows directly as object: 到北京 (to arrive in Beijing), 到家 (to get home), 到了 (arrived; here now). The 了 particle after 到 marks completion: the arrival has happened.</p>
      <p>In compounds with other verbs, 到 contributes its arrival semantics to produce words about reaching a goal or state: 做到 zuòdào (to accomplish — to do until arrival at the result), 说到 shuōdào (to mention — to speak until arriving at the topic), 达到 dádào (to reach, to attain — a formal compound of two near-synonyms). The pattern is always: action directed toward a point of contact or completion.</p>
    </div>

    <!-- ── RESULTATIVE COMPLEMENT ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="resultative"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">结果补语</span>
      <span class="sh-py">jiéguǒ bǔyǔ</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Resultative Complement — V + 到 = Achieved Action</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="到">
      <div class="scholar-label">语法洞见 yǔfǎ dòngjiàn · The Most Productive Grammar Point</div>
      <p>The resultative complement (结果补语 jiéguǒ bǔyǔ) is one of the defining features of Mandarin grammar. Chinese verbs do not inherently encode whether an action succeeded — that information is carried by a complement appended after the verb. 到 dào is the most frequent of all resultative complements. When 到 follows a verb, it adds: <em>the action reached its destination, made contact, succeeded.</em></p>
      <p>The logic is spatial and visceral. 到 means to arrive. When it follows a verb, it says: the action arrived at its goal, it got there, it landed. 听到 is listening that arrived — the sound reached you and registered. 看到 is looking that arrived — the image reached your awareness. The complement transforms an attempt into a confirmed result.</p>
      <p>Negation works through 没: 没听到 (didn't hear / hearing didn't arrive). Potential forms use the infixed 得/不: 听得到 (can hear it, hearing can arrive) vs. 听不到 (can't hear it, hearing cannot arrive). This potential form is extremely common in everyday speech.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">句型 jùxíng · V + 到 Pattern</span>
      <strong>Verb + 到</strong> = the verb's action reached completion, made contact, succeeded<br>
      <strong>Verb + 不到</strong> = the action cannot reach / cannot be achieved<br>
      <strong>Verb + 得到</strong> = the action can reach / can be achieved<br>
      <strong>没 + Verb + 到</strong> = the action did not reach / did not succeed (past)<br><br>
      听 tīng (listen) + 到 → <strong>听到</strong> tīngdào — to hear (listening that arrived)<br>
      看 kàn (look) + 到 → <strong>看到</strong> kàndào — to see (looking that arrived)<br>
      想 xiǎng (think) + 到 → <strong>想到</strong> xiǎngdào — to think of (thinking that arrived)<br>
      找 zhǎo (search) + 到 → <strong>找到</strong> zhǎodào — to find (searching that arrived)<br>
      学 xué (study) + 到 → <strong>学到</strong> xuédào — to learn / attain through study<br>
      感 gǎn (feel) + 到 → <strong>感到</strong> gǎndào — to feel / sense (feeling that arrived)<br>
      收 shōu (receive) + 到 → <strong>收到</strong> shōudào — to receive (receiving that arrived)
    </div>

    <!-- ── TIME & ENDPOINTS ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="time"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">时间与终点</span>
      <span class="sh-py">shíjiān yǔ zhōngdiǎn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Time &amp; Endpoints — 从…到… · 到底 · 到处</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="到">
      <div class="scholar-label">语法洞见 yǔfǎ dòngjiàn · 到 in Temporal Structure</div>
      <p>Beyond verbs of motion and perception, 到 marks the endpoint of time spans and the terminal point in spatial or logical progressions. The pattern 从 A 到 B (from A to B) covers both space and time with the same syntax. The arrival logic remains: 到 is the point where the journey ends.</p>
      <p>Two high-frequency compounds deserve special attention. 到底 dàodǐ (to the bottom) functions as an interrogative intensifier demanding a real answer: 你到底想要什么 (what do you really want, after all?). It presses the question until it reaches the ground. 到处 dàochù (to every place) means everywhere, all over — arrival has spread to every point.</p>
    </div>

        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">从…到…</span>
          <span class="card-py">cóng … dào …</span>
          <span class="card-en">from … to … (time or space)</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Pattern 句型 jùxíng</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          从 cóng (from; starting point) + A + 到 dào (arrive at; endpoint) + B. The standard Mandarin pattern for expressing ranges in both space and time. 从早到晚 (from morning to night), 从北京到上海 (from Beijing to Shanghai), 从小到大 (from childhood to adulthood). The two endpoints are held by 从 and 到 respectively, one anchoring origin, the other anchoring destination.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">她从早到晚都在工作。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā cóng zǎo dào wǎn dōu zài gōngzuò.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">She works from morning to night.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">从一数到十。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Cóng yī shǔ dào shí.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Count from one to ten.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">从中国到美国要飞多长时间？</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Cóng Zhōngguó dào Měiguó yào fēi duō cháng shíjiān?</div>
            <div class="ex-en">How long does it take to fly from China to America?</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-ochre">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">到底</span>
          <span class="card-py">dàodǐ</span>
          <span class="card-en">after all; in the end; (interrogative intensifier)</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Adv 副词 fùcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          到 dào (arrive at) + 底 dǐ (bottom; base). Arriving at the bottom: getting to the root of the matter, reaching the real answer. In interrogative sentences, 到底 intensifies the question and demands a genuine response — it presses the interlocutor to stop evading and give a straight answer. In declarative sentences, it means "after all" or "in the end" (a conclusion reached at the bottom of events).
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">你到底去不去？</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Nǐ dàodǐ qù bù qù?</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Are you going or not — really?</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这到底是怎么回事？</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhè dàodǐ shì zěnme huí shì?</div>
            <div class="ex-en">What is this really about? / What on earth is going on?</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">到底还是失败了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Dàodǐ háishì shībài le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">In the end, it still failed.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">用法 yòngfǎ · Interrogative vs. Declarative</span>
          到底 in questions = pressing for a real answer (impatient, insistent). 到底 in statements = "after all; when all is said and done" (conclusive). The spatial image — arriving at the bottom — works for both: the question reaches the bottom of the matter; the statement records what was found there.
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-teal">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">到处</span>
          <span class="card-py">dàochù</span>
          <span class="card-en">everywhere; all over; in every place</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Adv 副词 fùcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          到 dào (arrive at) + 处 chù (place; location). Arriving at every place: ubiquity, pervasiveness. 到处都是 (there are ... everywhere) is one of the most common constructions. 到处找 (to look everywhere) = searching that has spread to every location.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">春天来了，到处都是花。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Chūntiān lái le, dàochù dōu shì huā.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Spring has come — there are flowers everywhere.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我到处找你！</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ dàochù zhǎo nǐ!</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I've been looking everywhere for you!</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-violet">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">到现在</span>
          <span class="card-py">dào xiànzài</span>
          <span class="card-en">up to now; until now; so far</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Phrase 短语 duǎnyǔ</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          到 dào (arrive at) + 现在 xiànzài (now; the present). The endpoint is the present moment: a span of time that began in the past and has arrived at now. Used to describe situations, relationships, or states that have persisted up to this moment.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">到现在我们还没有收到回复。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Dào xiànzài wǒmen hái méiyǒu shōudào huífù.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Up to now we still haven't received a reply.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他们认识到现在已经二十年了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tāmen rènshi dào xiànzài yǐjīng èrshí nián le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">They have known each other for twenty years now.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">面面俱到</span>
        <span class="cy-py">miàn miàn jù dào</span>
        <span class="cy-en">every aspect covered; thorough in all directions</span>
        <span class="cy-note">面 miàn (face; surface; aspect) + 面 + 俱 jù (all; together) + 到 (arrived at). Arrival has reached every face, every surface, every angle of the matter. Used to praise comprehensive coverage — a plan that misses nothing, a speech that addresses every concern, an analysis that leaves no gap. Can carry a faintly ironic undertone when used of something overly cautious or bureaucratically exhaustive.</span>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">说到做到</span>
        <span class="cy-py">shuō dào zuò dào</span>
        <span class="cy-en">to do what you say; a person of their word</span>
        <span class="cy-note">说 shuō (to say) + 到 (arrive) + 做 zuò (to do) + 到 (arrive). Speech arrives, and then action arrives. The double 到 is the promise of two completions: what was said and what was done both reach their destination. Used to commend reliability and integrity — the person whose words and deeds are in complete alignment. The negation 说到做不到 (says it but can't follow through) is equally common as a criticism.</span>
      </div>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">无处不到</span>
        <span class="cy-py">wú chù bù dào</span>
        <span class="cy-en">all-pervading; present everywhere; reaching every corner</span>
        <span class="cy-note">无 wú (without; no) + 处 chù (place) + 不 bù (not) + 到 (arrive). There is no place that is not arrived at. The double negative — no place that has not been reached — produces the emphatic positive: total pervasiveness. Used of influence, power, information, or character that penetrates everywhere without exception. Classical register; found in philosophy, literature, and elevated prose.</span>
      </div>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">恰到好处</span>
        <span class="cy-py">qià dào hǎo chù</span>
        <span class="cy-en">just right; arriving at exactly the right point</span>
        <span class="cy-note">恰 qià (just; exactly) + 到 dào (arrive at) + 好 hǎo (good; right) + 处 chù (place; point). Arriving at precisely the right place: the perfectly calibrated amount, the perfectly timed remark, the execution that is neither too much nor too little. Used to describe seasoning in food, tone in conversation, force in an argument. One of the most graceful compliments in the language.</span>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="到">
      <div class="scholar-label">记忆法 jìyìfǎ · Master Retention Image</div>
      <p>An arrow in flight, and then: contact. The oracle bone form of 至 shows the exact moment — the shaft entering the ground, the horizontal surface receiving the tip. That instant of landing, of the gap between thrower and target closing to zero, is 到. It is not the throwing; it is the arrival.</p>
      <p>Hold that image when you encounter 到 as a resultative complement. 听到 — an arrow of sound that has reached its mark in your ear. 看到 — an arrow of attention that has struck the object and confirmed it. 想到 — a thought that was in flight and has now landed in the mind. 找到 — a search that was ranging across territory and has found its target. Every V + 到 compound is an arrow that has hit the ground. The action was launched; 到 confirms the landing.</p>
      <p>The potential forms make the image concrete: 听得到 means the arrow can reach you (you are within range, the sound can arrive). 听不到 means the arrow cannot reach you (out of range, the sound cannot arrive). When you ask 你听得到吗, you are asking: am I within your range? Has the distance closed?</p>
