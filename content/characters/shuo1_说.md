    <!-- ── ETYMOLOGY ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="etymology"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">字源</span>
      <span class="sh-py">zìyuán</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Etymology &amp; Structure</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="說">
      <div class="scholar-label">字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight</div>
      <p>Traditional form: <strong>說 = 言 yán</strong> (speech radical — a mouth with a horizontal line above, representing words emanating) <strong>+ 兑 duì</strong> (phonetic and semantic: to exchange, to convert; a mouth open with joy). The composition suggests: speech that converts or pleases — <em>words that land, that persuade, that bring delight.</em></p>
      <p>The simplified form replaces 言 with the abbreviated speech radical 讠, but 兑 remains. The 言 radical appears across the speech-and-thought character family: 话 huà (spoken words), 语 yǔ (language), 谢 xiè (to thank), 请 qǐng (to invite/request), 读 dú (to read aloud).</p>
      <p>说 covers an unusually wide semantic range: to say (我说 I say), to speak a language (说中文), to explain (说清楚 explain clearly), to scold (被妈妈说了一顿 was scolded by mom), and in its classical readings, to persuade (游说 shuì) and to be pleased (悦 yuè). One character, five conceptual domains.</p>
    </div>

    <!-- ── THREE READINGS ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="readings"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">三种读音</span>
      <span class="sh-py">sān zhǒng dúyīn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Three Readings: shuō / shuì / yuè</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">读音分工 dúyīn fēngōng · Reading Split</span>
      <strong>shuō (tone 1)</strong> → to speak, to say, to explain (everyday use)<br>
      <strong>shuì (tone 4)</strong> → to persuade (political/diplomatic: 游说 yóushuì = to lobby, to canvass)<br>
      <strong>yuè (tone 4)</strong> → to be pleased (classical only: 学而时习之，不亦说乎 — Analects opening line)
    </div>

    <div class="note">
      <span class="note-label">学者注 · Scholar Note</span>
      The yuè reading is famous from the Analects: 学而时习之，不亦<strong>说</strong>乎 — "To learn and regularly practice — is this not a pleasure?" Here 说 = 悦 yuè (delight). Classical texts use 说 for both shuō and yuè. The shuì reading (persuade) appears in Warring States political texts where itinerant persuaders (游说家) sold their counsel to rival states — the original lobbyists.
    </div>

    <!-- ── SPEECH VERBS ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="speech"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">言说动词</span>
      <span class="sh-py">yánshuō dòngcí</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Speech Verbs — Saying and Telling</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">说话</span>
          <span class="card-py">shuōhuà</span>
          <span class="card-en">to speak; to talk; to say something</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          说 shuō + 话 huà (spoken words; speech). The act of speaking in general — not the content but the activity. 会说话 = can speak / is good with words. 说话算数 = one's word is reliable (lit. speech-counts).
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">小孩子一岁开始说话。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Xiǎo háizi yī suì kāishǐ shuōhuà.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Small children start talking at age one.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他很会说话，让人舒服。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā hěn huì shuōhuà, ràng rén shūfu.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He's very good with words — he puts people at ease.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">听说</span>
          <span class="card-py">tīng shuō</span>
          <span class="card-en">I heard that; it is said that</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          听 tīng (to hear) + 说 shuō (speech). "I heard it said that…" — introduces hearsay or reported information. Softens a claim by flagging it as secondhand. Extremely common in conversation.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">听说他要去北京了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tīng shuō tā yào qù Běijīng le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I heard he's going to Beijing.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">说明</span>
          <span class="card-py">shuōmíng</span>
          <span class="card-en">to explain; to illustrate; explanation; instructions</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-vn">V/N 动名词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          说 shuō + 明 míng (clear, bright). To make something clear through speech. As a noun: 说明书 shuōmíngshū = instruction manual. As a verb: 这说明了… = "this demonstrates/shows that…"
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这说明他们之间有问题。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhè shuōmíng tāmen zhījiān yǒu wèntí.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">This shows there is a problem between them.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <!-- ── PERSUASION ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="persuasion"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">说服与辩论</span>
      <span class="sh-py">shuōfú yǔ biànlùn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Persuasion &amp; Argumentation</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">说服</span>
          <span class="card-py">shuōfú</span>
          <span class="card-en">to persuade; to convince</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          说 shuō + 服 fú (to submit; to be convinced; to be at someone's service). To speak until someone submits — to persuade successfully. The complement 服 marks the result: the persuasion worked.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他花了一个小时才说服她。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā huā le yī gè xiǎoshí cái shuōfú tā.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">It took him an hour to convince her.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">说不定</span>
          <span class="card-py">shuō bu dìng</span>
          <span class="card-en">maybe; hard to say; possibly</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Expr 表达 biǎodá</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          说 shuō + 不定 (cannot be fixed/determined). "Cannot be stated with certainty" — maybe, possibly. One of the most natural hedges in spoken Mandarin. More casual and spoken than 也许 yěxǔ or 可能 kěnéng.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">说不定他已经到了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Shuō bu dìng tā yǐjīng dào le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Maybe he's already arrived.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">成语</span>
      <span class="sh-py">chéngyǔ</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Idioms &amp; Set Phrases</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="chengyu-grid">

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">说一不二</span>
        <span class="cy-py">shuō yī bù èr</span>
        <span class="cy-en">one's word is final — what is said once stands without revision</span>
        <span class="cy-note">Lit: say-one-not-two. A person of absolute authority whose word is not open to revision or negotiation. Can be a compliment (decisive) or a criticism (inflexible) depending on context.</span>
      </div>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">口是心非</span>
        <span class="cy-py">kǒu shì xīn fēi</span>
        <span class="cy-en">the mouth says yes but the heart says no — saying one thing while thinking another</span>
        <span class="cy-note">Lit: mouth-is-yes heart-is-no. Hypocrisy or insincerity. The failure of 说 to match 心 (speech to align with heart) — a violation of Confucian authenticity (诚 chéng).</span>
      </div>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">有口难言</span>
        <span class="cy-py">yǒu kǒu nán yán</span>
        <span class="cy-en">having a mouth but unable to speak — in a position where one cannot explain oneself</span>
        <span class="cy-note">The frustration of having the means to speak but being unable to — because of social constraints, grief, or incriminating circumstances. One of the most vivid expressions for the experience of being misunderstood with no way to clear one's name.</span>
      </div>
