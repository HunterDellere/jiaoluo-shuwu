    <span class="section-anchor" id="overview"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><h2>The System</h2></div>
    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="医">
      <div class="scholar-label">中医 · zhōng yī</div>
      <p>中医 zhōngyī (中 = Chinese/central; 医 = medicine) is not a collection of folk remedies — it is a complete theoretical system developed over 2,500 years, with its own anatomy, physiology, pathology, diagnosis, and pharmacology. Its foundations are not empirical in the Western sense; they are cosmological: the human body is a microcosm of the universe, governed by the same principles of yin-yang, qi, and the five phases that govern everything else.</p>
      <p>Where Western medicine asks "what is broken and how do we fix it?", Chinese medicine asks "where is the flow disrupted, and how do we restore it?" This path starts with the foundational theory and builds toward practice.</p>
    </div>

      </div>

      <div class="card c-ochre">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">气 · Vital Force</span>
        </div>
        <p><a href="../../pages/characters/qi4_气.html">气 · Qi</a> — The vital force that circulates through the body along meridians. Understanding qi is prerequisite to understanding acupuncture, qigong, and diagnosis alike.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-ochre">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">五行 · Five Phases</span>
        </div>
        <p><a href="../../pages/science/topic_wuxing.html">五行 · The Five Phases</a> — Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water: the organizational framework that maps seasons, organs, emotions, flavours, and directions onto a single diagnostic grid.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-ochre">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">阴阳五行 · Philosophy</span>
        </div>
        <p><a href="../../pages/philosophy/topic_yinyang_wuxing.html">阴阳五行 · Yin-Yang & Five Phases</a> — The philosophical bedrock: how these two frameworks merged into the unified cosmological theory that underlies Chinese medicine, statecraft, and art.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-sienna">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">针灸 · Acupuncture</span>
        </div>
        <p><a href="../../pages/science/topic_zhenjiu.html">针灸 · Acupuncture and Moxibustion</a> — The two oldest physical interventions: needles that redirect qi through meridians, and the burning of mugwort to warm and tonify. Clinic, theory, and history together.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-sienna">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">本草 · Herbology</span>
        </div>
        <p><a href="../../pages/science/topic_bencao.html">本草 · Materia Medica</a> — The pharmacopoeia of Chinese medicine: thousands of plant, mineral, and animal substances classified by flavour, temperature, and channel affinity. The <em>Bencao Gangmu</em> of Li Shizhen is the canonical text.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-sienna">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">气功 · Cultivation</span>
        </div>
        <p><a href="../../pages/vocab/qigong_气功.html">气功 · Qigong</a> — Cultivating qi through coordinated movement, breath, and intention. Where acupuncture acts on the body from outside, qigong trains the practitioner to regulate their own flow from within.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-violet">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">中医 · Depth</span>
        </div>
        <p><a href="../../pages/science/topic_zhongyi.html">中医 · Clinical and Historical Depth</a> — The full scope: canonical texts, diagnostic methods (pulse, tongue, pattern differentiation), the history of the medicine from the Yellow Emperor's Classic to contemporary practice.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-red">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">风水 · Cosmology Applied</span>
        </div>
        <p><a href="../../pages/vocab/fengshui_风水.html">风水 · Feng Shui</a> — The same cosmological principles of qi, yin-yang, and the five phases applied to built space and landscape. A natural extension for readers who want to see how TCM theory radiates outward into Chinese civilisation at large.</p>
      </div>
