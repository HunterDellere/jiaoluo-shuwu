      <span class="topic-hero-eyebrow">Grammar · 语法 yǔfǎ · Conditional &amp; Restriction</span>
      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">只有…才</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">zhǐyǒu…cái</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">The restrictive conditional — only if the condition in 只有 is met, can the result in 才 occur. This frame expresses <em>necessity</em>: X is the one and only path to Y. Without X, Y is impossible.</p>
    </header>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="structure"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">核心结构</span><span class="sh-py">héxīn jiégòu</span><span class="sh-en">Core Structure</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">只有…才 框架 · Core Frame</span>
      <strong>只有 + condition + 才 + result</strong><br><br>
      只有努力，才能成功。 → Only by working hard can one succeed.<br>
      只有你去，我才放心。 → Only if you go will I feel at ease.<br>
      只有坚持到底，才能见到曙光。 → Only by persisting to the end can you see the dawn.
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head"><span class="card-cn">只有…才</span><span class="card-py">zhǐyǒu…cái</span><span class="card-en">only if…(then); only when…can</span></div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">Pattern 句型</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">只有 zhǐyǒu = only having; only if. 才 cái = then and only then. Together they create a restrictive logical relationship: the condition is <em>necessary</em>. Without it, the result cannot happen. This is stronger than 如果…就 (general if…then), because it rules out all other paths. Frequently appears in motivational and philosophical contexts.</div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example"><div class="ex-cn">只有经历过痛苦，才能体会幸福的珍贵。</div><div class="ex-py">Zhǐyǒu jīnglìguò tòngkǔ, cái néng tǐhuì xìngfú de zhēnguì.</div><div class="ex-en">Only by having experienced pain can you appreciate the preciousness of happiness.</div></div>
          <div class="example"><div class="ex-cn">只有互相信任，这段关系才能长久。</div><div class="ex-py">Zhǐyǒu hùxiāng xìnrèn, zhè duàn guānxi cái néng cháng jiǔ.</div><div class="ex-en">Only with mutual trust can this relationship last.</div></div>
        </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="logic"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">必要条件</span><span class="sh-py">bìyào tiáojiàn</span><span class="sh-en">Necessity vs. Sufficiency — The Logical Difference</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="才">
      <div class="scholar-label">语法洞见 yǔfǎ dòngjiàn · Grammar Insight</div>
      <p>只有…才 expresses a <strong>necessary condition</strong> (必要条件 bìyào tiáojiàn): X is required for Y, but X alone may not guarantee Y. "Only by studying hard can you pass" does not mean studying hard is sufficient; other factors may still prevent passing. It means: not studying = definitely not passing.</p>
      <p>This contrasts with 只要…就 (sufficient condition), where "as long as X, then Y" — X is enough to bring about Y. In practice, 只有…才 says "X is the minimum requirement" and 只要…就 says "X is all you need." Chinese speakers switch between them with precision to signal whether they are describing a floor or a threshold.</p>
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="cai"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">才的角色</span><span class="sh-py">cái de juésè</span><span class="sh-en">The Role of 才 — Consequence Marker</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="才">
      <div class="scholar-label">才 cái · Multiple Functions</div>
      <p>才 cái carries multiple meanings depending on context. In 只有…才, it functions as a <strong>consequence marker</strong> meaning "then and only then." 才 has two other core uses: (1) <strong>lateness / delay</strong>: 你才来 = you're only now arriving (I've been waiting); (2) <strong>talent</strong>: 才能 cáinéng = talent and ability.</p>
      <p>In the conditional frame, 才 always comes before the main verb of the result clause and cannot be dropped. Compare: 只有努力才能成功 vs. *只有努力能成功 (wrong). 才 is structurally required to mark the contingent result. It signals that this result is not freely available — it requires the condition just stated.</p>
    </div>
