    <header class="topic-hero">
      <span class="topic-hero-eyebrow">Grammar · 语法 yǔfǎ</span>
      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">的话</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">de huà</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">A two-character phrase placed at the end of a condition clause — the most flexible and colloquial way to say "if" in Mandarin.</p>
    </header>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="overview"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">概览</span>
      <span class="sh-py">gàilǎn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">What 的话 Does</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="话">
      <div class="scholar-label">语法洞见 yǔfǎ dòngjiàn · Grammar Insight</div>
      <p><strong>的话 de huà</strong> literally means "this talk / this matter," but as a grammatical particle it marks a phrase as a <em>conditional premise</em>: "in the case that X; if X." It is placed at the <em>end</em> of the if-clause, immediately before the result clause. This is the opposite of English, where "if" comes at the beginning.</p>
      <p>的话 is distinctively <strong>colloquial</strong>. It belongs firmly to spoken Mandarin and casual written language (text messages, informal emails). In formal writing, 如果…就 or 假如…则 are preferred. The feel of 的话 is soft and tentative, presenting the condition gently without insisting on the result.</p>
      <p>的话 can follow almost any type of clause: noun phrases, verb phrases, full sentences. Its flexibility makes it the most useful conditional marker for everyday conversation.</p>
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="basic"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">基本</span>
      <span class="sh-py">jīběn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Basic Pattern</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">基本句型 jīběn jùxíng · Core Pattern</span>
      <strong>[Condition clause] + 的话, [Result clause]</strong><br>
      你有时间的话，来我家吃饭。If you have time, come to my place for dinner.<br>
      天气好的话，我们去爬山。If the weather is good, let's go hiking.
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">Clause + 的话 + Result</span>
          <span class="card-py">clause + de huà</span>
          <span class="card-en">If ___; in the case that ___</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Structure 句型 jùxíng</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          的话 is placed after the condition. The result clause follows, often introduced by 就 jiù (then) or 可以 kěyǐ (can). The subject of the result clause may or may not be expressed; if it's the same as the condition clause, it is typically dropped.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">你不舒服的话，就在家休息吧。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Nǐ bù shūfu de huà, jiù zài jiā xiūxi ba.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">If you're not feeling well, just rest at home.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">明天下雨的话，我们就取消计划。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Míngtiān xià yǔ de huà, wǒmen jiù qǔxiāo jìhuà.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">If it rains tomorrow, we'll cancel the plan.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">你喜欢的话，可以带走。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Nǐ xǐhuān de huà, kěyǐ dài zǒu.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">If you like it, you can take it with you.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这件事难的话，我来帮你。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhè jiàn shì nán de huà, wǒ lái bāng nǐ.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">If this is difficult, I'll help you.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">语法 yǔfǎ · The Role of 就</span>
          就 jiù frequently appears in the result clause — it functions as a logical connector: "then; in that case." 如果/的话… 就 is one of the most common conditional pairs in Mandarin. But 就 is not obligatory — the conditional meaning is clear from 的话 alone.
        </div>
      </div>

    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">搭配</span>
      <span class="sh-py">dāpèi</span>
      <span class="sh-en">With 如果 / 要是 — Strengthening the Condition</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">条件词搭配 tiáojiàn cí dāpèi · Conditional Pairing Patterns</span>
      <strong>如果…的话,…就…</strong> — formal/neutral: If… then…<br>
      <strong>要是…的话,…就…</strong> — more colloquial, slightly warmer register<br>
      <strong>假如…的话,…就…</strong> — formal, hypothetical register<br>
      <strong>Clause + 的话</strong> — 的话 alone, most conversational
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">如果 / 要是 + … + 的话</span>
          <span class="card-py">rúguǒ / yàoshi + … + de huà</span>
          <span class="card-en">If (doubled conditional) — emphasis</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Structure 句型 jùxíng</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          Placing both 如果/要是 at the beginning AND 的话 at the end is a common intensification that frames the condition more explicitly and adds a conversational flavor. Neither marker alone is required; both together are emphatic and natural.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">如果你明天有空的话，我们去看电影吧。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Rúguǒ nǐ míngtiān yǒu kòng de huà, wǒmen qù kàn diànyǐng ba.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">If you're free tomorrow, let's go see a movie.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">要是你不想去的话，就不用勉强。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Yàoshi nǐ bù xiǎng qù de huà, jiù bùyòng miǎnqiǎng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">If you don't want to go, there's no need to force yourself.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="话">
      <div class="scholar-label">语域 yǔyù · When to Use 的话</div>
      <p>的话 is primarily a <strong>spoken-register</strong> marker. It is natural and warm in conversation, and extremely common in everyday Mandarin. It appears in:</p>
      <p>✓ Casual conversation · ✓ Text messages and social media · ✓ Informal emails · ✓ Dialogue in fiction</p>
      <p>In formal writing (official documents, academic papers, news reporting), 如果 alone or 假如/倘若 are preferred. 的话 would feel out of place in a government report or academic abstract.</p>
      <p>的话 can also soften a request or instruction. 你方便的话，帮我一个忙 "If it's convenient for you, help me with something." The 的话 signals that you recognize the other person's agency and are not demanding.</p>
    </div>
