      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">如果…就</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">rúguǒ … jiù</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">The standard conditional frame in Mandarin: "If [condition], then [result]." Covers real-world conditions, hypotheticals, advice, and warnings.</p>
    </header>

    <!-- ── ETYMOLOGY ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="etymology"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">字源</span>
      <span class="sh-py">zìyuán</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Etymology — 如果 and 就 Unpacked</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="如">
      <div class="scholar-label">字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight</div>
      <p><strong>如 rú</strong> (to be like; to match; to follow) + <strong>果 guǒ</strong> (fruit; result; indeed). 如 originally depicted a woman kneeling before a mouth, speech conforming to another's. 果 shows a tree bearing fruit, the natural outcome of growth. Together: "if [this condition] indeed comes to pass" — <em>if indeed, supposing.</em></p>
      <p><strong>就 jiù</strong> is one of Mandarin's most versatile words. It marks the consequence of a condition, immediacy ("right away"), and limitation ("just/only"). As a consequence marker, 就 picks up where 如果 leaves off: the condition having been granted, 就 says "then and only then." It ties the result tightly to the premise.</p>
      <p>Chinese conditionals are structurally similar to English "if…then" but with a crucial difference: 就 is an <em>adverb</em> that sits before the verb in the consequence clause, not a conjunction. Understanding this prevents learners from omitting it or placing it in the wrong position.</p>
    </div>

    <!-- ── STRUCTURE ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="structure"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">句型框架</span>
      <span class="sh-py">jùxíng kuàngjià</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Structure — The Conditional Frame</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">基本框架 jīběn kuàngjià · Basic Frame</span>
      <strong>[Subject] + 如果 [condition clause]，[Subject] + 就 [consequence clause]</strong><br><br>
      Same subject: 如果你去，你就会后悔。(If you go, you'll regret it.)<br>
      Different subjects: 如果你去，我就不去。(If you go, I won't.)<br>
      No 如果 (just 就): 你去，我就不去。(You go, I won't.) — casual spoken shorthand
    </div>

    <div class="note">
      <span class="note-label">语法 yǔfǎ · Subject Position</span>
      When both clauses share a subject, the subject comes <em>before</em> 如果, not after: <strong>你如果去，就会后悔</strong>. If different subjects, each follows its clause head. Misplacing the subject is the most common learner error with this pattern.
    </div>

            <div class="ex-py">Rúguǒ míngtiān xià yǔ, wǒmen jiù bù qù le.</div>

          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">如果你不努力，就会落后。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Rúguǒ nǐ bù nǔlì, jiù huì luòhòu.</div>

      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">如果…就 (hypothetical)</span>
          <span class="card-py">rúguǒ … jiù</span>
          <span class="card-en">if (counterfactual / wish) … then</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Conj 连词 liáncí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">

            <div class="ex-cn">如果我有钱，就买那栋房子了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Rúguǒ wǒ yǒu qián, jiù mǎi nà dòng fángzi le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">If I had money, I would have bought that house.</div>
          </div>

            <div class="ex-py">Rúguǒ shíjiān kěyǐ dào liú, wǒ yīdìng bù huì nàme zuò.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">If time could flow backward, I definitely wouldn't have done that.</div>

    <!-- ── THE ROLE OF 就 ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="jiu"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">就的作用</span>
      <span class="sh-py">jiù de zuòyòng</span>
      <span class="sh-en">The Role of 就 — Consequence Marker</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="就">
      <div class="scholar-label">语法洞见 yǔfǎ dòngjiàn · Grammar Insight</div>
      <p>就 jiù is doing heavy grammatical lifting in this construction. As an adverb, it precedes the verb in the consequence clause and means "then and in that case." It signals: the condition having been met, this result follows <em>naturally and directly.</em></p>
      <p>Importantly, 就 can appear even without 如果: 你早点来，我就早点走 (if/when you come earlier, I'll leave earlier). The condition-result relationship is implied by the sequential structure alone. 就 marks the result. In spoken Mandarin, learners frequently drop 如果 while keeping 就, and that's grammatically valid.</p>
      <p>Be aware: 就 has many other uses (just; only; right away; as early as). In the conditional frame it is specifically a consequence marker, and a prior condition clause is what signals that function.</p>
    </div>
