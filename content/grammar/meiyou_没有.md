    <header class="topic-hero">
      <span class="topic-hero-eyebrow">Grammar · 语法 yǔfǎ</span>
      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">没有</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">méi yǒu</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">The negation of possession and past action — and the critical rule that separates 没 from 不.</p>
    </header>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="overview"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">概览</span>
      <span class="sh-py">gàilǎn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Overview — The Two Negators</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="没">
      <div class="scholar-label">语法洞见 yǔfǎ dòngjiàn · Grammar Insight</div>
      <p>Mandarin has two primary negation words, and they are <strong>not interchangeable:</strong></p>
      <p><strong>不 bù</strong> is the <em>volitional, general, or present-state</em> negator. It negates willingness, habits, ongoing states, and non-past actions, expressing what won't happen, isn't true by nature, or is refused.</p>
      <p><strong>没 méi</strong> is the <em>factual, past-event, or possession</em> negator. It negates completed actions, possession of things, and objective facts about what has or hasn't occurred, expressing what didn't happen or doesn't exist in fact.</p>
      <p>The distinction is fundamentally about <strong>time and volition</strong>: 不 looks to the present/future or expresses will; 没 looks to the past or states facts about existence and occurrence.</p>
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="possession"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">没有</span>
      <span class="sh-py">méiyǒu</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Negating Possession — "Don't Have"</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">没有 méiyǒu · Possession Negation Pattern</span>
      <strong>Subject + 没有 + Object</strong><br>
      我没有钱。I don't have money. ／ 他没有时间。He doesn't have time. ／ 这里没有人。There's nobody here.
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">没有 + Noun</span>
          <span class="card-py">méiyǒu + N</span>
          <span class="card-en">don't have ___; there is no ___</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 negation</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          没有 is the negation of 有 (to have; there is/are). 有 can <strong>never</strong> be negated with 不 — only 没. This is one of the most common errors for English speakers: *不有 is ungrammatical. Always say 没有.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我没有手机。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ méiyǒu shǒujī.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I don't have a mobile phone.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">冰箱里没有食物了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Bīngxiāng lǐ méiyǒu shíwù le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">There's no more food in the fridge.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他没有钱，但是很快乐。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā méiyǒu qián, dànshì hěn kuàilè.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He has no money, but he's very happy.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">关键规则 guānjiàn guīzé · Critical Rule</span>
          有 yǒu is negated ONLY with 没, never 不:<br>
          ✓ 我没有时间 "I don't have time"<br>
          ✗ 我不有时间 (ungrammatical)<br>
          This applies to existential 有 as well: 没有人 "there is nobody" / 没有问题 "there is no problem."
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="past"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">过去</span>
      <span class="sh-py">guòqù</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Negating Past Actions — "Didn't / Haven't"</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">没(有) + Verb · Past Negation Pattern</span>
      <strong>Subject + 没(有) + Verb (+ Object)</strong><br>
      我没去。I didn't go. ／ 他没吃饭。He didn't eat. ／ 我们没有看那部电影。We didn't watch that film.
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">没(有) + Verb</span>
          <span class="card-py">méi(yǒu) + V</span>
          <span class="card-en">didn't ___; haven't ___</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 past negation</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          In past-tense contexts, 没 (or 没有) negates the completion of an action. Note: when 没 is used in this way, the completion particle 了 le is <strong>dropped</strong> — 没 and 了 are incompatible. 没 takes the place of 了, indicating the action did not complete.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我昨天没去上课。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ zuótiān méi qù shàngkè.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I didn't go to class yesterday.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他没有告诉我这件事。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā méiyǒu gàosu wǒ zhè jiàn shì.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He didn't tell me about this.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我没睡好。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ méi shuì hǎo.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I didn't sleep well.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">语法 yǔfǎ · 没 + 了 incompatibility</span>
          ✓ 他吃了饭。"He ate." (completed action + 了)<br>
          ✓ 他没吃饭。"He didn't eat." (negated with 没, no 了)<br>
          ✗ 他没吃了饭。(ungrammatical — cannot combine 没 and 了 this way)<br>
          The 了 is simply absent when 没 negates a completed action.
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="experience"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">经历</span>
      <span class="sh-py">jīnglì</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Negating Experience — 没 + Verb + 过</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">没 + Verb + 过</span>
          <span class="card-py">méi + V + guo</span>
          <span class="card-en">have never ___; haven't ___ before</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 experiential negation</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          过 guo marks experiential aspect — that something has ever occurred as part of one's life experience. 没 + Verb + 过 negates that experience: the action has never happened at all. Compare: 我没去 "I didn't go [that time]" vs. 我没去过 "I've never been there."
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我没去过中国。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ méi qùguo Zhōngguó.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I've never been to China.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">她没吃过北京烤鸭。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā méi chīguo Běijīng kǎoyā.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">She's never eaten Peking Duck.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我没见过这么漂亮的地方。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ méi jiànguò zhème piàoliang de dìfāng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I've never seen such a beautiful place.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="bu-vs-mei"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">辨析</span>
      <span class="sh-py">biànxī</span>
      <span class="sh-en">不 vs. 没 — The Critical Distinction</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">不 vs. 没 · The Core Decision</span>
      Use <strong>不</strong> for: (1) present/future negation; (2) habitual states; (3) willingness/refusal; (4) characteristics ("I'm not a teacher")<br>
      Use <strong>没</strong> for: (1) past actions that didn't complete; (2) negating 有 (possession/existence); (3) negating life experience (+ 过)
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">不 vs. 没 minimal pairs</span>
          <span class="card-py">bù vs. méi</span>
          <span class="card-en">Seeing the difference clearly</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">辨析 biànxī contrast</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          The same verb with 不 and 没 can produce very different meanings. The choice reveals whether you're talking about facts (past/existence) or dispositions/futures (will/habit).
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我不吃肉。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ bù chī ròu.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I don't eat meat. (habit / dietary rule / won't)</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我没吃肉。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ méi chī ròu.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I didn't eat (any) meat. (fact about a past occasion)</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他不来了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā bù lái le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He's decided not to come. (change of plan / refusal)</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他没来。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā méi lái.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He didn't come. (factual — he was absent)</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="patterns"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">句型</span>
      <span class="sh-py">jùxíng</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Common 没有 Patterns to Memorize</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-n">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">没什么</span>
          <span class="card-py">méi shénme</span>
          <span class="card-en">nothing / it's nothing / no big deal</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">Set phrase</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          没 + 什么 (what; anything). 没什么 = "nothing; it's nothing." As a response to thanks or apologies: 没什么！= "No worries! / It's nothing!" Also as a statement: 冰箱里没什么吃的 "There's nothing much to eat in the fridge."
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">谢谢你！— 没什么，小事一件。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Xièxie nǐ! — Méi shénme, xiǎo shì yī jiàn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Thank you! — It's nothing, just a small thing.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-n">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">没关系</span>
          <span class="card-py">méiguānxi</span>
          <span class="card-en">it doesn't matter; no problem; never mind</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">Set phrase</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          没 + 关系 guānxi (connection; relationship; relevance). "There is no [problematic] connection." The universal Chinese reassurance: "It's fine / don't worry about it." Response to apologies, inconveniences, or expressions of guilt.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">对不起！— 没关系，没事的。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Duìbuqǐ! — Méiguānxi, méi shì de.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I'm sorry! — It's fine, don't worry.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">还没(有)呢</span>
          <span class="card-py">hái méi(yǒu) ne</span>
          <span class="card-en">not yet</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Set phrase</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          还 hái (still; yet) + 没有 + 呢 ne (continuation particle). The standard way to say "not yet," implying the action is expected but hasn't completed. Stronger and more colloquial than simply saying 没有.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他来了吗？— 还没有呢。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā lái le ma? — Hái méiyǒu ne.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Has he arrived? — Not yet.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">你吃饭了吗？— 还没呢。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Nǐ chīfàn le ma? — Hái méi ne.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Have you eaten? — Not yet.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
