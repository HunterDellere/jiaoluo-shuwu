    <header class="topic-hero">
      <span class="topic-hero-eyebrow">Grammar · 语法 yǔfǎ</span>
      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">得</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">de · (neutral tone)</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">The structural particle that bridges an action to its result or quality — how well, how fast, how beautifully something was done.</p>
    </header>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="overview"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">概览</span>
      <span class="sh-py">gàilǎn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">What 得 Does</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="得">
      <div class="scholar-label">语法洞见 yǔfǎ dòngjiàn · Grammar Insight</div>
      <p>In Mandarin, the particle <strong>得 de</strong> (neutral tone) marks the beginning of a <em>degree complement</em> (程度补语 chéngdù bǔyǔ) or a <em>result/potential complement</em>. This page covers the degree complement — describing <em>how well or in what manner an action was performed.</em></p>
      <p>The core pattern is <strong>V + 得 + Description</strong>: the verb comes first, then 得, then an adjective or phrase that evaluates the quality of that verb. "She sings well" → 她唱得很好. "He runs fast" → 他跑得很快. "They spoke [in a way that was] very lively" → 他们说得很生动.</p>
      <p>Note: 得 here is NOT the same as 得 dé (to get; to obtain) or 得 děi (must; have to). All three are written identically but are different words. Context and tone disambiguate.</p>
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="basic"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">基本</span>
      <span class="sh-py">jīběn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Basic Pattern — V + 得 + Adj</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">基本句型 jīběn jùxíng · Core Pattern</span>
      <strong>Subject + Verb + 得 + Adjective/Description</strong><br>
      她唱得很好。She sings well. ／ 他说得太快了。He speaks too fast. ／ 孩子睡得很香。The child is sleeping soundly.
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">V + 得 + Adj</span>
          <span class="card-py">V + de + Adj</span>
          <span class="card-en">verbs in a manner that is ___</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Structure 句型 jùxíng</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          得 bridges the verb to a phrase describing the quality or degree of the action. The description after 得 can be an adjective, adverb + adjective, or a clause. The description modifies the <em>manner</em> of the action — not the action itself.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">她写得很漂亮。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā xiě de hěn piàoliang.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">She writes beautifully. (Her writing is beautiful.)</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他们跑得非常快。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tāmen pǎo de fēicháng kuài.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">They run extremely fast.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这道菜做得真好吃！</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhè dào cài zuò de zhēn hǎochī!</div>
            <div class="ex-en">This dish is cooked really deliciously!</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">老师讲得很清楚。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Lǎoshī jiǎng de hěn qīngchǔ.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">The teacher explains very clearly.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">语法 yǔfǎ · Key Point</span>
          The adverb 很 hěn is usually required before adjectives in complements (unless another adverb replaces it). ✓ 写得很好 ✓ 写得非常好 ✓ 写得不好 — but ✗ 写得好 (sounds incomplete without a following contrast). Exception: 极了 and similar intensifiers don't need 很: 写得好极了 "written superbly."
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="object"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">宾语</span>
      <span class="sh-py">bīnyǔ</span>
      <span class="sh-en">With Objects — The Repeated Verb</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">带宾语的句型 dài bīnyǔ de jùxíng · Pattern with Object</span>
      When the verb has an object, the verb must be repeated: <strong>Subject + V + Object + V + 得 + Adj</strong><br>
      Or alternatively: <strong>Subject + Object + V + 得 + Adj</strong> (topic-comment structure)
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">V + Obj + V + 得 + Adj</span>
          <span class="card-py">repeated-verb pattern</span>
          <span class="card-en">The verb repetition pattern</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Structure 句型 jùxíng</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          When the verb takes a direct object, 得 cannot follow both verb and object. Solution: repeat the verb after the object, then attach 得. Alternatively, move the object to topic position before the subject.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">她说中文说得很流利。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā shuō Zhōngwén shuō de hěn liúlì.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">She speaks Chinese fluently. (verb repeated)</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他踢足球踢得非常好。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā tī zúqiú tī de fēicháng hǎo.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He plays football extremely well. (verb repeated)</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">中文她说得很流利。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhōngwén tā shuō de hěn liúlì.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Chinese — she speaks it fluently. (topic-comment)</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">语法 yǔfǎ · Why Repeat?</span>
          In Chinese grammar, the degree complement 得+Adj must directly follow a bare verb (no object between them). The object creates a gap, so the verb is repeated to "re-attach" 得. Both the repeated-verb form and the topic-comment form are correct and common.
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="questions"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">问句</span>
      <span class="sh-py">wènjù</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Questions &amp; Negation</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">V + 得 + 怎么样？</span>
          <span class="card-py">V + de + zěnmeyàng?</span>
          <span class="card-en">How well does/did ___ do ___?</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Question pattern</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          怎么样 zěnmeyàng (how; what's it like?) is the standard question word for degree complements. 你说得怎么样？ "How well do you speak it?"
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他唱得怎么样？— 唱得很好！</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā chàng de zěnmeyàng? — Chàng de hěn hǎo!</div>
            <div class="ex-en">How does he sing? — He sings very well!</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">考试考得怎么样？— 考得还可以。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Kǎoshì kǎo de zěnmeyàng? — Kǎo de hái kěyǐ.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">How did the exam go? — It went okay.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">V + 得 + 不 + Adj (negation)</span>
          <span class="card-py">V + de + bù + Adj</span>
          <span class="card-en">does not ___ in a ___ way</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Negation pattern</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          To negate, place 不 before the adjective in the complement: 写得不好 "writes not well" · 说得不清楚 "speaks not clearly." The 不 negates the quality, not the verb. ✓ 写得不好 ✗ 不写得好 (wrong position).
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他今天跑得不太快。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā jīntiān pǎo de bù tài kuài.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He didn't run very fast today.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这道题我做得不对。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhè dào tí wǒ zuò de bù duì.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I got this problem wrong.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="adverb"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">副词</span>
      <span class="sh-py">fùcí</span>
      <span class="sh-en">地 vs. 得 — Modifying the Verb vs. Evaluating the Result</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">地 vs. 得 · The Critical Distinction</span>
      <strong>Adj + 地 + Verb</strong> → adverb before the verb (how you do something as you do it)<br>
      认真地学习 "to study diligently" — 地 describes your manner while studying<br><br>
      <strong>Verb + 得 + Adj</strong> → degree complement after the verb (evaluation of the result)<br>
      学习得很认真 "studied in a diligent way" — 得 evaluates how the studying came out
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="得">
      <div class="scholar-label">辨析 biànxī · 地/得 Minimal Pair</div>
      <p><strong>她快乐地唱歌。</strong> Tā kuàilè de chànggē. — She sings happily. <em>(She is happy while singing — 地 modifies the manner)</em></p>
      <p><strong>她歌唱得很好。</strong> Tā gēchàng de hěn hǎo. — She sings very well. <em>(Her singing results in being good — 得 evaluates the outcome)</em></p>
      <p>The distinction is time and viewpoint: 地 describes the manner <em>during</em> the action; 得 evaluates the <em>result or quality</em> of the action. In speech they sound identical (both are neutral-tone "de") — context and position clarify.</p>
    </div>
