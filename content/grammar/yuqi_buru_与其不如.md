      <span class="topic-hero-eyebrow">Grammar · 语法 yǔfǎ · Paired Construction 成对连词</span>
      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">与其…不如</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">yǔqí … bùrú</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">The preference-comparison frame: "Rather than [X], it would be better to [Y]." Rejects one option in favor of a superior one — one of the most rhetorically powerful constructions in Mandarin.</p>
    </header>

    <!-- ── ETYMOLOGY ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="etymology"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">字源</span>
      <span class="sh-py">zìyuán</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Etymology — 与其 and 不如 Unpacked</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="与">
      <div class="scholar-label">字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight</div>
      <p><strong>与其 yǔqí</strong> literally means "compared with that" or "to be together with [the option of]." 与 yǔ = with, together with; 其 qí = that, it (third-person classical pronoun). Together: "compared with that [option]…," the construction sets up one option for rejection.</p>
      <p><strong>不如 bùrú</strong> literally means "not as good as" or "does not measure up to." 不 bù = not; 如 rú = to be like, to match, to equal (如 originally depicted a woman kneeling before a mouth, speech being matched to another's). So 不如 = "doesn't match up to" → "is inferior to" → "it is better to do instead." The idiom 不如意 (not as wished) and the proverb 百闻不如一见 (hearing a hundred times does not equal seeing once) use 如 with the same logic.</p>
      <p>The full construction is a classical Chinese framing that passed into modern Mandarin intact. Its formal elegance makes it common in writing, speeches, and any situation requiring polished argument.</p>
    </div>

    <!-- ── STRUCTURE ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="structure"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">句型框架</span>
      <span class="sh-py">jùxíng kuàngjià</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Structure — How to Build the Frame</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">基本框架 jīběn kuàngjià · Basic Frame</span>
      <strong>与其 [Option A]，不如 [Option B]</strong><br>
      "Rather than [A], it would be better to [B]"<br><br>
      <strong>与其 [A]，倒不如 [B]</strong> — colloquial reinforcement<br>
      <strong>与其说 [A]，不如说 [B]</strong> — "rather than saying A, it would be more accurate to say B"
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="与">
      <div class="scholar-label">句型解析 jùxíng jiěxī · Frame Analysis</div>
      <p><strong>与其</strong> introduces the <em>rejected</em> option, the thing you are choosing not to do. It is placed at the beginning of the first clause. The subject, if present, usually comes <em>after</em> 与其 (与其你去，不如我去: rather than you going, better if I go). If there is only one subject, it can come before 与其: 你与其担心，不如行动.</p>
      <p><strong>不如</strong> introduces the <em>preferred</em> option. It marks the pivot point: everything before 不如 is the rejected option, everything after is the recommendation. The rhetorical weight lands on 不如 and what follows.</p>
      <p>The optional variant <strong>与其说…不如说</strong> is particularly elegant: it reframes a statement rather than an action. "Rather than saying this is a failure, it would be more accurate to say it is a lesson."</p>
    </div>

    <!-- ── EXAMPLES ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="examples"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">例句</span>
      <span class="sh-py">lìjù</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Examples — Simple to Complex</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">与其等待，不如行动</span>
          <span class="card-py">yǔqí děngdài, bùrú xíngdòng</span>
          <span class="card-en">rather than waiting, it's better to act</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Conj 连词 liáncí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          The simplest pattern — verb phrases on both sides. 与其 + action to reject, 不如 + action to recommend. The rhetorical punch: waiting is rejected as the inferior choice; action is endorsed.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">与其坐在家里担心，不如出去找工作。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Yǔqí zuò zài jiā lǐ dānxīn, bùrú chūqù zhǎo gōngzuò.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Rather than sitting at home worrying, it's better to go out and look for work.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">与其抱怨，不如想想怎么改变。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Yǔqí bàoyuàn, bùrú xiǎngxiǎng zěnme gǎibiàn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Rather than complaining, it's better to think about how to change things.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">与其说…不如说</span>
          <span class="card-py">yǔqí shuō … bùrú shuō</span>
          <span class="card-en">rather than saying … it would be more accurate to say</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Conj 连词 liáncí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          A sophisticated variant for reframing a characterization. Instead of rejecting an action, it rejects one way of <em>describing</em> something and proposes a more accurate one. High frequency in essays, criticism, and intellectual discourse.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">与其说他是失败了，不如说他得到了宝贵的教训。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Yǔqí shuō tā shì shībài le, bùrú shuō tā dédào le bǎoguì de jiàoxùn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Rather than saying he failed, it would be more accurate to say he gained a valuable lesson.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">与其说这是一部爱情片，不如说是一部哲学电影。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Yǔqí shuō zhè shì yī bù àiqíngpiàn, bùrú shuō shì yī bù zhéxué diànyǐng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Rather than calling this a romance film, it would be more accurate to call it a philosophical film.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">语体 yǔtǐ · Register</span>
          与其说…不如说 is a hallmark of sophisticated written Chinese: essays, book reviews, film criticism, and editorials.
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="variants"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">变体与近义</span>
      <span class="sh-py">biàntǐ yǔ jìnyì</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Variants — 宁可 / 宁愿 / 倒不如</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">宁可…也不</span>
          <span class="card-py">nìngkě … yě bù</span>
          <span class="card-en">would rather … than (not)</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Conj 连词 liáncí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          宁可 nìngkě (would rather; be willing to) + action + 也不 (even so, not). Expresses a strong preference: even at a cost, one would rather do X than Y. More emotionally intense than 与其不如; suggests willingness to sacrifice.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我宁可一个人住，也不和那个人同屋。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ nìngkě yīgè rén zhù, yě bù hé nàge rén tóng wū.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I would rather live alone than share a room with that person.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">宁愿…也不</span>
          <span class="card-py">nìngyuàn … yě bù</span>
          <span class="card-en">would willingly … rather than</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Conj 连词 liáncí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          宁愿 nìngyuàn (would willingly prefer; 愿 = willing) — very similar to 宁可 but slightly softer. Expresses a genuine desire/preference rather than just reluctant acceptance. Often interchangeable with 宁可 in modern usage.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">她宁愿饿着，也不愿意吃那些食物。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā nìngyuàn è zhe, yě bù yuànyì chī nàxiē shíwù.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">She would rather go hungry than eat that food.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    <div class="note" style="margin-top:1rem;">
      <span class="note-label">关键区别 · Key Distinction</span>
      与其…不如 is <em>rational recommendation</em>: the speaker is choosing the better option and inviting others to agree. 宁可/宁愿…也不 is <em>personal resolve</em>: the speaker expresses a willingness to accept a cost for their preference. The former is used in argument; the latter in statements of conviction. They cannot be swapped without changing the register and emotional tone.
