      <span class="topic-hero-eyebrow">Grammar · 语法 yǔfǎ · Degree &amp; Change 程度变化</span>
      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">越来越</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">yuèláiyuè</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">The continuous escalation pattern: a quality or state growing steadily over time. "More and more cold," "increasingly popular," "getting harder and harder."</p>
    </header>

    <!-- ── ETYMOLOGY ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="etymology"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">字源</span>
      <span class="sh-py">zìyuán</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Etymology — 越 to Surpass, to Cross Over</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="越">
      <div class="scholar-label">字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight</div>
      <p><strong>越 yuè</strong> = 走 zǒu (to move) + 戉 yuè (a battle-axe: something that cuts through). To move by cutting through: to cross, to surpass, to go beyond a boundary. 越 is used for crossing rivers (越过), exceeding limits (越界), and surpassing standards (越来越 = surpassing further and further, "more and more").</p>
      <p>In 越来越, the structure is: 越 (surpass) + 来 (come) + 越 (surpass). The 来 here functions as a linking element, not the directional "come." The tripling encodes a continuous crossing of thresholds: each time, the bar is cleared and a new one set. 越来越 describes an active, ongoing process of exceeding, not a fixed state.</p>
      <p>越 also appears in the name of the ancient state of Yue 越国 (in modern Zhejiang/Fujian) and in the name for Vietnam (越南 Yuènán — south of Yue).</p>
    </div>

    <!-- ── STRUCTURE ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="structure"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">句型框架</span>
      <span class="sh-py">jùxíng kuàngjià</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Structure — 越来越 + Adj/Verb</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">基本框架 jīběn kuàngjià · Basic Frame</span>
      <strong>[Subject] + 越来越 + [Adj/Verb]</strong> (+ 了 optional, confirms change)<br><br>
      天气越来越冷了。The weather is getting colder and colder.<br>
      他的中文越来越好。His Chinese is getting better and better.<br>
      我越来越喜欢这里。I like it here more and more.
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">越来越 + adjective</span>
          <span class="card-py">yuèláiyuè + adj</span>
          <span class="card-en">getting more and more [adj]</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Pattern 句型 jùxíng</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          The most common pattern. 越来越 before a stative adjective describes a gradual change in degree over time. The optional 了 at the end confirms that the change is ongoing or has taken effect.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这个城市越来越大了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhège chéngshì yuèláiyuè dà le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">This city is getting bigger and bigger.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">她的声音越来越小，最后消失了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā de shēngyīn yuèláiyuè xiǎo, zuìhòu xiāoshī le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Her voice got quieter and quieter, and finally disappeared.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">学中文越来越有意思。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Xué Zhōngwén yuèláiyuè yǒu yìsi.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Learning Chinese is getting more and more interesting.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">语法 yǔfǎ · Important Constraint</span>
          越来越 cannot be used with adjectives of fixed, inherent properties. You cannot say 越来越四 (more and more "four") — the quality must be gradable. Also, 越来越 does not use 很 or 非常 before the adjective: ✗ 越来越很好. The 越来越 itself carries degree intensification.
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">越来越 + verb</span>
          <span class="card-py">yuèláiyuè + V</span>
          <span class="card-en">doing [verb] more and more</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Pattern 句型 jùxíng</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          越来越 also works before verbs that describe mental states or incremental behaviors: 越来越喜欢 (like more and more), 越来越担心 (worry more and more), 越来越依赖 (rely on more and more).
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我越来越觉得他说的有道理。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ yuèláiyuè juéde tā shuō de yǒu dàolǐ.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I increasingly feel that what he says makes sense.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <!-- ── 越…越 FRAME ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="yueyue"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">越…越</span>
      <span class="sh-py">yuè … yuè</span>
      <span class="sh-en">The 越…越 Frame — The More X, the More Y</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">越…越 框架 · The Correlative Frame</span>
      <strong>[Subject] + 越 [condition/action] + 越 [result/state]</strong><br><br>
      越吃越胖 — the more you eat, the fatter you get<br>
      越说越乱 — the more it's explained, the more confused it gets<br>
      越想越难受 — the more I think about it, the worse I feel
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-vn">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">越…越</span>
          <span class="card-py">yuè … yuè</span>
          <span class="card-en">the more … the more</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Pattern 句型 jùxíng</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          A correlative frame where one increasing quality drives another. The first 越 is tied to the condition/cause; the second to the escalating result. Both sides must be gradable. This structure is extremely productive and versatile, a signature pattern of fluent Chinese.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">她越唱越好听。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā yuè chàng yuè hǎotīng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">The more she sings, the better it sounds.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这本书越看越有意思。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhè běn shū yuè kàn yuè yǒu yìsi.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">The more you read this book, the more interesting it gets.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他越紧张，说得越不好。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā yuè jǐnzhāng, shuō de yuè bù hǎo.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">The more nervous he gets, the worse he speaks.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">否定 fǒudìng · Negation Pattern</span>
      <strong>越来越不 + [adj/verb]</strong> — increasingly less; less and less<br><br>
      越来越不好 — getting worse and worse<br>
      越来越不想去 — less and less willing to go<br>
      越来越不像话 — increasingly out of line
