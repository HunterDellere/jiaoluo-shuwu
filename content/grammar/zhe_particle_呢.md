    <header class="topic-hero">
      <span class="topic-hero-eyebrow">Grammar · 语法 yǔfǎ</span>
      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">呢</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">ne · (neutral tone)</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">A neutral-tone particle that opens a sentence outward: returning a question to the asker, softening a wh-question, or marking a state as still in progress.</p>
    </header>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="overview"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">概览</span>
      <span class="sh-py">gàilǎn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">What 呢 Does</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="呢">
      <div class="scholar-label">语法洞见 yǔfǎ dòngjiàn · Grammar Insight</div>
      <p><strong>呢 ne</strong> is a sentence-final modal particle, pronounced in the neutral tone (轻声 qīngshēng). It belongs to the same family as 吧 ba, 啊 a, 嘛 ma, and 吗 ma, all of which sit at the end of a sentence to shape its pragmatic force without changing its propositional content.</p>
      <p>呢 has four core uses in modern Mandarin: returning a question to the original asker (你呢？ "And you?"), softening a wh-question into something more curious or wondering (他在哪儿呢？ "Where is he, I wonder?"), marking a state or action as ongoing (他在睡觉呢 "He's sleeping right now"), and topicalizing a noun phrase (我呢，不太喜欢咖啡 "As for me, I don't really like coffee"). The thread connecting all four is open-endedness: 呢 leaves the sentence breathing rather than closing it.</p>
      <p>Crucially, 呢 cannot form a yes/no question on its own. *你是学生呢？ is wrong; the correct yes/no question is 你是学生吗？ The most common learner mistake is reaching for 呢 in the slot where 吗 belongs.</p>
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="reciprocal"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">反问</span>
      <span class="sh-py">fǎnwèn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Function 1 · Reciprocal Questions (And you?)</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">反问用法 fǎnwèn yòngfǎ · Reciprocal Pattern</span>
      <strong>NP + 呢？</strong> → returns the previous question to the named subject<br>
      你呢？And you? ／ 他呢？What about him? ／ 你的家人呢？How about your family?
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">NP + 呢？</span>
          <span class="card-py">NP + ne?</span>
          <span class="card-en">And ___? What about ___? How about ___?</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Particle 语气词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          The most frequent use of 呢 in everyday speech. After someone has asked or stated something, you reply, then use NP + 呢？ to bounce the same question back. The previous context supplies the predicate: only the new subject is named.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">A：你叫什么名字？B：我叫王明，你呢？</div>
            <div class="ex-py">A: Nǐ jiào shénme míngzi? B: Wǒ jiào Wáng Míng, nǐ ne?</div>
            <div class="ex-en">A: What's your name? B: I'm Wang Ming, and you?</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我喝茶，你呢？</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ hē chá, nǐ ne?</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I'll have tea. What about you?</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">你妈妈在家，你爸爸呢？</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Nǐ māma zài jiā, nǐ bàba ne?</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Your mum is home; what about your dad?</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">语境 yǔjìng · Context Required</span>
          NP + 呢？ is meaningless on its own. The predicate has to be supplied by the previous sentence. The opener 你呢？ to a stranger out of the blue is wrong; it needs a preceding statement to anchor it.
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="wh-soft"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">疑问</span>
      <span class="sh-py">yíwèn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Function 2 · Softened Wh-Questions</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">疑问用法 yíwèn yòngfǎ · Wh-Question Pattern</span>
      <strong>Wh-question + 呢？</strong> → adds curiosity, mild puzzlement, or musing<br>
      他在哪儿呢？Where is he? (wondering) ／ 你说什么呢？What are you saying? (puzzled) ／ 怎么办呢？What should we do?
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">Wh-Question + 呢？</span>
          <span class="card-py">Wh + ne?</span>
          <span class="card-en">Wonders, muses, or softens a wh-question</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Particle 语气词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          Attached to an existing wh-question (with 谁 shéi, 什么 shénme, 哪儿 nǎr, 怎么 zěnme, 为什么 wèishénme). Without 呢 the question is direct and information-seeking. With 呢 it becomes wondering, musing, or sometimes mildly exasperated. The speaker is half asking the listener and half thinking aloud.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我的钥匙在哪儿呢？</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ de yàoshi zài nǎr ne?</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Now where are my keys?</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这个问题怎么办呢？</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhège wèntí zěnme bàn ne?</div>
            <div class="ex-en">What are we going to do about this?</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他为什么不回我的消息呢？</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā wèishénme bù huí wǒ de xiāoxi ne?</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Why won't he reply to my messages?</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="continuing"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">持续</span>
      <span class="sh-py">chíxù</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Function 3 · Continuing State (still going on)</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">持续用法 chíxù yòngfǎ · Ongoing Pattern</span>
      <strong>(在 / 正在 +) Verb + (着) + 呢</strong> → marks the action or state as in progress right now<br>
      他在睡觉呢。He's sleeping right now. ／ 我吃饭呢。I'm eating. ／ 外面下着雨呢。It's raining outside.
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-ochre">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">V (+ 着) + 呢</span>
          <span class="card-py">V (+ zhe) + ne</span>
          <span class="card-en">is/are ___-ing (right now); ___ is going on</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Particle 语气词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          呢 at the end of a verb phrase reinforces that the action is ongoing at the moment of speaking. It often pairs with the durative aspect marker 着 zhe or the progressive 在 / 正在. On its own, 呢 alone can also do the job in casual speech: 我看书呢 "I'm reading."
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">A：你在做什么？B：我做饭呢。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">A: Nǐ zài zuò shénme? B: Wǒ zuò fàn ne.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">A: What are you doing? B: I'm cooking.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">小声点，孩子睡着呢。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Xiǎoshēng diǎn, háizi shuì zhe ne.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Keep your voice down; the kid is asleep.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">外面正下着大雨呢，别出门。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wàimiàn zhèng xià zhe dà yǔ ne, bié chūmén.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">It's pouring outside right now; don't go out.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">辨析 biànxī · 呢 vs 着 vs 在</span>
          <strong>着 zhe</strong> is the durative aspect marker attached to the verb (睡着 "asleep"). <strong>在 / 正在</strong> is the progressive adverb before the verb (在睡 "is sleeping"). <strong>呢</strong> is the sentence-final particle that adds the "right now, as we speak" flavor. They stack: 他正在睡着呢. Each layer can be added or dropped depending on how vivid you want the immediacy.
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="topic"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">话题</span>
      <span class="sh-py">huàtí</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Function 4 · Topic Marking (As for X)</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-teal">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">NP + 呢，clause</span>
          <span class="card-py">NP + ne, clause</span>
          <span class="card-en">As for ___, ; ___, well, </span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Particle 语气词</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          Mid-sentence rather than sentence-final. 呢 follows a topicalized noun phrase and signals "as for this particular thing, here's what I think." Slightly informal and conversational; common in spoken Mandarin when the speaker is comparing two cases or singling one out.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">茶呢，我喜欢绿茶；咖啡呢，我不太喝。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Chá ne, wǒ xǐhuān lǜchá; kāfēi ne, wǒ bú tài hē.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">As for tea, I like green tea; as for coffee, I don't drink much of it.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">这件事呢，我们慢慢商量。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zhè jiàn shì ne, wǒmen mànman shāngliáng.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">As for this matter, let's talk it over slowly.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">说到工作呢，他做得很认真。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Shuō dào gōngzuò ne, tā zuò de hěn rènzhēn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">When it comes to work, he's really diligent.</div>
          </div>
        </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="contrast"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">辨析</span>
      <span class="sh-py">biànxī</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Contrast · 呢 vs 吗 vs 吧 vs 啊</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">四粒子比较 sì lìzǐ bǐjiào · Four Particles Compared</span>
      <strong>吗</strong> ma → forms a yes/no question, no presupposition (你是学生吗？ Are you a student?)<br>
      <strong>呢</strong> ne → returns a question, softens a wh-question, marks ongoing state; cannot form yes/no questions<br>
      <strong>吧</strong> ba → seeks shared ground: confirmation, suggestion, hedged assertion (你是学生吧？ You're a student, right?)<br>
      <strong>啊</strong> a → emotional reaction or urging (你来啊！ You came! / Come on!)
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="呢">
      <div class="scholar-label">练习 liànxí · Practice Contrast</div>
      <p>Take the same fragment <strong>你是学生</strong> "you are a student" and watch the particles do different work:</p>
      <p><strong>你是学生吗？</strong> = Are you a student? (genuine open question)<br>
      <strong>你是学生吧？</strong> = You're a student, right? (speaker already thinks so, asks to confirm)<br>
      <strong>你是学生啊！</strong> = Oh, you're a student! (surprise or recognition)<br>
      <strong>你呢？</strong> = And you? (only meaningful after a previous statement; never *你是学生呢？)</p>
      <p>The yes/no question slot belongs to 吗. 呢 fills the other slots: returning, wondering, continuing, topicalizing. Hearing native speakers shift between 吗 and 呢 in quick succession is one of the fastest ways to feel where the line falls.</p>
    </div>
