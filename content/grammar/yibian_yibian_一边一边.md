      <span class="topic-hero-eyebrow">Grammar · 语法 yǔfǎ · Aspect &amp; Simultaneity</span>
      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">一边…一边</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">yībiān…yībiān</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">The frame for doing two things at once. 一边 V₁ 一边 V₂ = while doing V₁, also doing V₂. One subject, two simultaneous ongoing actions — the Mandarin construction for multitasking.</p>
    </header>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="structure"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">核心结构</span><span class="sh-py">héxīn jiégòu</span><span class="sh-en">Core Structure</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">一边…一边 框架 · Core Frame</span>
      <strong>Subject + 一边 + V₁ + 一边 + V₂</strong><br><br>
      他一边吃饭一边看手机。 → He eats while looking at his phone.<br>
      我一边听音乐一边做作业。 → I do homework while listening to music.<br>
      她一边走一边想。 → She thinks as she walks.
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head"><span class="card-cn">一边…一边</span><span class="card-py">yībiān…yībiān</span><span class="card-en">while doing…; at the same time as</span></div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Pattern 句型</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">Both verbs happen simultaneously, by the same subject. Neither action is the "main" one; they are co-occurring. 一边 derives from 边 biān (side; edge; border): literally "on one side…on another side," two actions running in parallel lanes. Both V₁ and V₂ are typically ongoing or habitual actions, not instantaneous ones.</div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example"><div class="ex-cn">她一边唱歌一边跳舞。</div><div class="ex-py">Tā yībiān chànggē yībiān tiàowǔ.</div><div class="ex-en">She sings while dancing.</div></div>
          <div class="example"><div class="ex-cn">他们一边喝茶一边聊天。</div><div class="ex-py">Tāmen yībiān hē chá yībiān liáotiān.</div><div class="ex-en">They chat while drinking tea.</div></div>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="rules"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">主语与动词规则</span><span class="sh-py">zhǔyǔ yǔ dòngcí guīzé</span><span class="sh-en">Subject &amp; Verb Rules</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="边">
      <div class="scholar-label">语法要点 yǔfǎ yàodiǎn · Grammar Points</div>
      <p><strong>Same subject required.</strong> Both actions must share the same subject. 一边…一边 cannot connect two different people doing different things. For different subjects, use 用…的时候, 在…的同时, or simply two separate clauses. This is the single most important constraint.</p>
      <p><strong>Action verbs, not stative.</strong> 一边…一边 works with action verbs (走, 吃, 唱, 工作, 看). It does not combine well with stative verbs (是, 有, 知道, 喜欢); you can't use it for two states that simply coexist. Use 而且 or 既…又 instead.</p>
      <p><strong>Subject position.</strong> The subject appears once, before the first 一边. Do not repeat the subject after the second 一边.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">变体形式</span><span class="sh-py">biàntǐ xíngsī</span><span class="sh-en">Variants — 边…边 and 一面…一面</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

      <table>
        <thead><tr><th>形式 Form</th><th>语域 Register</th><th>说明 Notes</th></tr></thead>
        <tbody>
          <tr><td class="td-cn">一边…一边</td><td class="td-py">spoken; written</td><td>Standard form. Works in all registers.</td></tr>
          <tr><td class="td-cn">边…边</td><td class="td-py">casual spoken</td><td>Abbreviated form, drops the 一. Equivalent meaning, more colloquial. 边走边说 = talk while walking.</td></tr>
          <tr><td class="td-cn">一面…一面</td><td class="td-py">written; formal</td><td>More literary, more common in formal writing and news. Functionally identical to 一边…一边.</td></tr>
          <tr><td class="td-cn">一方面…另一方面</td><td class="td-py">formal / analytical</td><td>On one hand…on the other hand. Used for presenting two aspects of an argument, not simultaneous physical actions.</td></tr>

      <table>

        <tbody>
          <tr><td class="td-cn">一边…一边</td><td>while doing; simultaneously</td><td>Two ongoing ACTIONS by the same subject happening at the same time.</td></tr>
          <tr><td class="td-cn">又…又</td><td>both…and; at once (adj)</td><td>Two qualities or states simultaneously: 又快又好 (fast and good), 又哭又笑 (crying and laughing).</td></tr>
