    <header class="topic-hero">
      <span class="topic-hero-eyebrow">Grammar · 语法 yǔfǎ</span>
      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">已经</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">yǐ jīng</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">Already — an adverb placed before the verb, typically paired with 了 at the end of the sentence to confirm completed or changed states.</p>
    </header>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="overview"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">概览</span>
      <span class="sh-py">gàilǎn</span>
      <span class="sh-en">What 已经 Does</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="经">
      <div class="scholar-label">语法洞见 yǔfǎ dòngjiàn · Grammar Insight</div>
      <p><strong>已经 yǐjīng</strong> — 已 yǐ (already; to have completed — a classical particle) + 经 jīng (to pass through; via; already — as in 经过 jīngguò "to pass through"). Together: "already having passed through" — the state has been reached, the process has been completed. The adverb is placed <em>before</em> the verb in Mandarin (unlike English "already," which can go in many positions).</p>
      <p>已经 asserts that a state, event, or action has been accomplished before the moment of speaking. It typically pairs with 了 at the end of the clause — the 了 signals the completion or state change that 已经 sets up. But 了 is not always required: with stative verbs or in questions, 了 may be absent.</p>
      <p>已经 is an adverb — it modifies the verb phrase, never a noun or adjective directly. Its position: always after the subject but before the verb (and any adverb of degree). If 了 is present in the sentence, it goes at the end of the verb phrase or sentence.</p>
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="position"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">位置</span>
      <span class="sh-py">wèizhi</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Position Rules</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">基本句型 jīběn jùxíng · Core Position</span>
      <strong>[Subject] + 已经 + [Verb Phrase] + 了</strong><br>
      我已经吃了。I've already eaten.<br>
      他已经到了。He's already arrived.<br>
      她已经学了三年中文了。She has already studied Chinese for three years.
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">已经 + Verb + 了</span>
          <span class="card-py">yǐjīng + V + le</span>
          <span class="card-en">Have already [done]; [state] has already occurred</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Structure 句型 jùxíng</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          已经 precedes the verb; 了 follows the verb phrase or closes the sentence. The two work together: 已经 provides the "already" meaning; 了 confirms the completion or change of state. Both can appear together (most common with action verbs) or 了 alone can imply "already" without 已经 (especially in past-tense narratives).
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">我已经买票了，你不用买了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Wǒ yǐjīng mǎi piào le, nǐ bùyòng mǎi le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">I've already bought tickets — you don't need to buy any.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">火车已经开走了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Huǒchē yǐjīng kāi zǒu le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">The train has already left.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他已经是大学生了。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā yǐjīng shì dàxuéshēng le.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He is already a university student. (state change — no action verb)</div>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="note">
          <span class="note-label">语法 yǔfǎ · With Stative Verbs</span>
          With stative verbs (是, 有, adjectives used predicatively), 了 confirms a new state rather than completed action: 已经很晚了 "It's already very late" — 很晚 (very late) is a state; 了 signals that this state has now been reached.
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="withLe"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">与了</span>
      <span class="sh-py">yǔ le</span>
      <span class="sh-en">已经 + 了 — The Natural Pairing</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">已经…了 with Duration or Quantity</span>
      When stating duration with 已经 + time expression + 了, the sentence often has <strong>two 了</strong>:<br>
      我已经学了三年中文了。I've already been studying Chinese for three years.<br>
      First 了 = action completion marker (after verb) · Second 了 = sentence-final state change<br>
      Both 了 are present and grammatically distinct in function.
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="negative"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">否定</span>
      <span class="sh-py">fǒudìng</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Negation — 还没(有)</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">否定句型 fǒudìng jùxíng · Not Yet</span>
      The negative of 已经 is <strong>还没(有)</strong> hái méi(yǒu) — "not yet":<br>
      我已经吃了。→ 我还没(有)吃。I haven't eaten yet.<br>
      他已经来了。→ 他还没(有)来。He hasn't come yet.<br>
      Do NOT use 不已经 or 已经不 — use 还没(有) for the "not yet" negation.
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">还没(有) + Verb</span>
          <span class="card-py">hái méi(yǒu) + V</span>
          <span class="card-en">Not yet — the negative counterpart of 已经</span>
        </div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">Structure 句型 jùxíng</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">
          还 hái (still; yet) + 没有 méiyǒu (have not). Together: "still have not" = "not yet." The verb that follows is NOT completed — this is the non-completion marker, and no 了 is added. 还没吃 "haven't eaten yet" · 还没来 "hasn't come yet" · 还没准备好 "not ready yet." The 有 in 没有 is often dropped in speech: 还没来 is more common than 还没有来.
        </div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">作业还没做完，不能出去玩。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Zuòyè hái méi zuò wán, bù néng chūqù wán.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">The homework isn't finished yet — you can't go out to play.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">你去过北京吗？——还没有，打算明年去。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Nǐ qùguò Běijīng ma? — Hái méiyǒu, dǎsuàn míngnián qù.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Have you been to Beijing? — Not yet, I'm planning to go next year.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>
