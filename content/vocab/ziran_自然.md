      <span class="topic-hero-eyebrow">Vocab · 词汇 cíhuì</span>
      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">自然</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">zì rán</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">Self-so: the things that function as they are, without outside interference — and in modern Chinese, "the natural world" and "of course" simultaneously.</p>
    </header>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="etymology"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">字源</span>
      <span class="sh-py">zìyuán</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Etymology: 自 + 然</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="自">
      <div class="scholar-label">自 (self, nose) + 然 (thus; burning) = "of itself, thus"</div>
      <p>自 (zì) is a pictograph of a nose. The oracle bone form shows a face in profile with the nose prominently drawn. In early Chinese, pointing to one's own nose was the gesture for "I/me/self," the same logic that makes the nose the symbol of the self. 自 carries the meaning of "self, oneself" and also "from, since" in classical grammar.</p>
      <p>然 (rán) originally depicted meat being roasted over a fire: 肰 (meat) + 火 (fire). The burning gave 然 the sense of "being so, being the case, thus." Classical 然 functions as "yes, so, thus," an affirmation that something is as it is. The modern suffix 然 appears across dozens of abstract adjectives: 突然 (suddenly), 当然 (naturally, of course), 显然 (obviously).</p>
      <p>Put together, 自然 means "of itself, thus": the state of things that are simply as they are without external compulsion or intervention. The compound appears first in pre-Qin philosophical texts and carries this philosophical weight into every one of its modern uses. When a Chinese speaker says 自然 to mean "of course," they are, without knowing it, invoking the same concept Laozi used for the ground of the cosmos.</p>
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="daoist"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">道法自然</span>
      <span class="sh-py">dào fǎ zìrán</span>
      <span class="sh-en">The Daoist Concept</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="道">
      <div class="scholar-label">道法自然 — the Way takes 自然 as its model</div>
      <p>Chapter 25 of the Daodejing contains the line that made 自然 a philosophical concept rather than merely a compound adjective: <strong>人法地，地法天，天法道，道法自然</strong> — "Humanity models itself on Earth, Earth models itself on Heaven, Heaven models itself on the Way, the Way models itself on what is so of itself." The chain ascends through human civilization, the physical world, the cosmic principle, and then arrives at 自然: not as something above the Way, but as the Way's own character. It does not take a model from elsewhere. It is its own ground.</p>
      <p>This was Laozi's most radical claim. Every other thing in the hierarchy takes something above it as a model; only 自然 has no external reference. 自然 is the condition of things that function without outside enforcement, without the need for law, command, or correction. The Daoist political ideal is the ruler who governs so well that the people do not know they are being governed (无为而治, wú wéi ér zhì). For the full treatment of Daoist philosophy, see the <a href="../philosophy/topic_laozi.html">老子 Laozi</a> entry.</p>
      <p>The connection to 道德 is direct: if the Way models itself on 自然, then virtue (德) is the natural functioning of things in alignment with the Way. Virtue, on this reading, is not an imposition from outside but the expression of what things already are. See the <a href="daode_道德.html">道德</a> entry for the relationship between Dao and De.</p>
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="modern"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">现代用法</span>
      <span class="sh-py">xiàndài yòngfǎ</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Modern Usage: Three Grammatical Roles</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">自然 in modern Mandarin</span>
      <p><strong>Noun:</strong> 自然界 (zìránjiè, the natural world), 大自然 (dà zìrán, nature at large, capitalized nature). Subject or object in sentences about the physical environment: 保护自然 (protect nature), 自然灾害 (natural disaster).</p>
      <p><strong>Adjective:</strong> Something that is natural, unforced, unstilted. 他的表演很自然 means "His performance was very natural." This use carries the philosophical root: the acting feels unforced because it follows what the character already is.</p>
      <p><strong>Adverb:</strong> "Naturally, of course, it goes without saying." 你做好了准备，自然就不会紧张 means "If you've prepared well, you naturally won't be nervous." 自然 in this use signals a logical consequence that follows without requiring further argument.</p>
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="compounds"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">自然的词</span>
      <span class="sh-py">zìrán de cí</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Key Compounds</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">
      <div class="card c-teal">
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">n</span></div>
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">大自然</span>
          <span class="card-py">dà zìrán</span>
          <span class="card-en">Nature (the natural world)</span>
        </div>
        <p class="card-def">大 (great, grand) + 自然 (nature). The capitalized sense of Nature as a whole: the natural world in its entirety, contrasted with the human-built world. 亲近大自然 means "to get close to nature." The 大 prefix gives 自然 the scope of a proper noun, equivalent to English "Mother Nature" minus the personification.</p>
        <div class="examples">
          <span class="ex-cn">我们应该保护大自然。</span>
          <span class="ex-py">Wǒmen yīnggāi bǎohù dà zìrán.</span>
          <span class="ex-en">We should protect the natural world.</span>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="card c-ochre">
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">n</span></div>
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">自然界</span>
          <span class="card-py">zìránjiè</span>
          <span class="card-en">the natural world; the realm of nature</span>
        </div>
        <p class="card-def">自然 + 界 (jiè, realm, domain). More formal and analytical than 大自然. Used in scientific, educational, and official contexts: 自然界的规律 means "the laws of the natural world." 界 gives it the sense of a defined domain, the way 政界 (political circles) or 学界 (academic circles) carve out sectors.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card c-red">
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">n</span></div>
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">自然灾害</span>
          <span class="card-py">zìrán zāihài</span>
          <span class="card-en">natural disaster</span>
        </div>
        <p class="card-def">自然 (natural) + 灾害 (zāihài, disaster, calamity). Floods, earthquakes, typhoons, droughts. The standard administrative and media term. China's disaster relief system uses 自然灾害 as the formal category encompassing all events caused by natural rather than human forces.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="card c-violet">
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-adj">adj</span></div>
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">自然而然</span>
          <span class="card-py">zìrán ér rán</span>
          <span class="card-en">naturally and inevitably; of its own accord</span>
        </div>
        <p class="card-def">自然 + 而 (connective, "and thus") + 然 (so, thus). A reinforced form of 自然 meaning something happened completely on its own, without any forcing or planning. 习惯成自然，自然而然就好了 means "habit becomes second nature; it naturally and inevitably gets easier." 而然 adds emphasis: not just natural, but unavoidably, organically so.</p>
        <div class="examples">
          <span class="ex-cn">时间长了，这些词自然而然就记住了。</span>
          <span class="ex-py">Shíjiān cháng le, zhèxiē cí zìrán ér rán jiù jì zhù le.</span>
          <span class="ex-en">Given enough time, these words will naturally stick on their own.</span>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="card c-sienna">
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-adv">adv</span></div>
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">当然</span>
          <span class="card-py">dāngrán</span>
          <span class="card-en">of course; naturally; certainly</span>
        </div>
        <p class="card-def">当 (dāng, to be, to fit, to be appropriate) + 然 (so, thus). "Being so as it should be." 当然 is the standard spoken equivalent of 自然 in its "of course" sense: where 自然 as an adverb feels slightly literary, 当然 is completely colloquial and appears dozens of times daily in natural conversation. Both share 然 as their root.</p>
        <div class="examples">
          <span class="ex-cn">当然可以！</span>
          <span class="ex-py">Dāngrán kěyǐ!</span>
          <span class="ex-en">Of course you can!</span>
        </div>
      </div>
