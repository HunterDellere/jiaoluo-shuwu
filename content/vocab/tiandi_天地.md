      <span class="topic-hero-eyebrow">Vocabulary · 词汇 cíhuì</span>
      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">天地</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">tiāndì</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">Heaven and earth , the two poles of the Chinese cosmos, the space within which all things arise, and the subject of the most consequential disagreement in Chinese philosophy.</p>
    </header>

    <!-- ── ETYMOLOGY ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="etymology"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">字源</span>
      <span class="sh-py">zìyuán</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Etymology &amp; the Cosmic Triad</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="天">
      <div class="scholar-label">字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight</div>
      <p><strong>天 tiān</strong> in oracle bone script shows a human figure with an enlarged, emphasized head , the sky as the space above the person, the great canopy overhead. The head is the topmost part of the body; heaven is the topmost part of the world. From this concrete image of "above the human" came the extended senses: sky, heaven, the divine order, fate, the natural course of things.</p>
      <p><strong>地 dì</strong> combines <strong>土</strong> (earth, soil) as the semantic component with <strong>也</strong> as a phonetic indicator. Earth as the solid ground beneath , the surface of accumulation, the receiver of rain, the support for all life. Where 天 is ascent and canopy, 地 is foundation and spread.</p>
      <p>Together as <strong>天地</strong>, the two characters name the totality of the created order: everything that exists falls between the dome of heaven above and the ground of earth below. The space between them is the space of existence itself. Chinese cosmology does not stop at this dyad. The complete picture requires a third term: <strong>人</strong> rén, the human. The triad <strong>天地人</strong> (tiān dì rén) , Heaven, Earth, and the Human , places the person not as a spectator of the cosmos but as a constitutive element within it. The human being is not outside nature looking in; the human stands between heaven and earth as the third force that completes the order.</p>
    </div>

    <!-- ── DAOIST READING ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="daoist"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">道家读法</span>
      <span class="sh-py">dàojiā dúfǎ</span>
      <span class="sh-en">The Daoist Reading — Laozi &amp; 刍狗</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="道">
      <div class="scholar-label">经典 jīngdiǎn · Dàodéjīng, Chapter 5</div>
      <p>The most consequential invocation of 天地 in Chinese literature is Laozi's opening line of chapter 5:</p>
      <p><strong>天地不仁，以万物为刍狗。</strong><br>
      <em>Tiāndì bù rén, yǐ wànwù wéi chúgǒu.</em><br>
      "Heaven and Earth are not benevolent; they treat the ten thousand things as straw dogs."</p>
      <p><strong>刍狗 chúgǒu</strong> requires unpacking. These were grass figures shaped like dogs, used in ritual sacrifices in ancient China. Before the ceremony, they were treated with great care , wrapped in silk, placed on the altar, prepared with ceremony. After the sacrifice was complete, they were discarded without a second thought, walked over, used as kindling. They received meticulous care when needed for the ritual purpose, then were abandoned when the purpose had passed.</p>
      <p>Laozi's image is precise: Heaven and Earth are not cruel , they are equanimous. They do not favor anything. The ten thousand things (万物 wànwù, everything in existence) arise in their season and pass away in their season, and the cosmos treats their arising and passing with the same impartial indifference it gives to straw dogs before and after a ceremony. This is a direct corrective to any reading of heaven as a moral patron. The Confucian tendency to see cosmic order as moral endorsement of human hierarchy meets, in Laozi, a cosmos that simply operates without it.</p>
      <p>The line continues: <strong>圣人不仁，以百姓为刍狗。</strong> (The sage likewise is not benevolent; he treats the hundred clans as straw dogs.) The ideal ruler, like Heaven and Earth, does not favor, does not intervene, does not impose moral categories on those he governs. He allows things to follow their own nature.</p>
    </div>

    <!-- ── CONFUCIAN READING ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="confucian"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">儒家读法</span>
      <span class="sh-py">rújiā dúfǎ</span>
      <span class="sh-en">The Confucian Reading — Zhang Zai &amp; the Western Inscription</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>
    </div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="仁">
      <div class="scholar-label">学者洞见 xuézhě dòngjiàn · Zhang Zai, 西铭 Xī Míng (c. 1076 CE)</div>
      <p>Zhang Zai (张载, 1020–1077 CE), the Song dynasty Neo-Confucian philosopher, opens his <em>Western Inscription</em> (西铭 Xī Míng) with one of the most quoted passages in Chinese philosophy:</p>
      <p><strong>乾称父，坤称母；予兹藐焉，乃混然中处。</strong><br>
      <em>Qián chēng fù, Kūn chēng mǔ; yǔ zī miǎo yān, nǎi hùn rán zhōng chǔ.</em><br>
      "Heaven I call Father, Earth I call Mother; I, so small, dwell at the center of their mingling."</p>
      <p><strong>乾 Qián</strong> is the Heaven trigram of the Yijing, pure Yang; <strong>坤 Kūn</strong> is the Earth trigram, pure Yin. Zhang Zai names them Father and Mother , not as metaphor but as ontological claim. All people are siblings, produced by the same cosmic parents. All things are companions within the one household of heaven and earth. The ethical consequence Zhang Zai draws: to serve others is to serve heaven and earth, and to harm others is to harm the family home.</p>
      <p>Where Laozi's 天地 is indifferent , not cruel, not benevolent, simply generative and letting-pass , Zhang Zai's 天地 is constitutively moral and familial. The space between heaven and earth is not the space of impartial arising and passing; it is the shared household of all humanity, and ethics is housekeeping. Both readings have been available in Chinese thought for over two thousand years. The tension between them is not resolved; it is inhabited.</p>
    </div>

    <!-- ── KEY COMPOUNDS ── -->
    <span class="section-anchor" id="compounds"></span>
    <div class="section-head">
      <span class="sh-cn">词汇</span>
      <span class="sh-py">cíhuì</span>
      <span class="sh-en">Key Compounds &amp; Expressions</span>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-teal">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">天地间</span>
          <span class="card-py">tiāndì jiān</span>
          <span class="card-en">between heaven and earth; in this world</span>
        </div>
        <div class="card-def">间 jiān (between; the space between two things). 天地间 names the space of human existence. A frequent location phrase in classical and literary prose.</div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">天地间有许多我们无法解释的事。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tiāndì jiān yǒu xǔduō wǒmen wúfǎ jiěshì de shì.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Between heaven and earth, there are many things we cannot explain.</div>
          </div>
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">天地间最难的事，莫过于了解自己。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tiāndì jiān zuì nán de shì, mò guò yú liǎojiě zìjǐ.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Of all things between heaven and earth, nothing is harder than knowing oneself.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-ochre">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">天地人</span>
          <span class="card-py">tiān dì rén</span>
          <span class="card-en">Heaven, Earth, Human — the cosmic triad; the Three Powers</span>
        </div>
        <div class="card-def">The completed cosmological triad (三才 sān cái). Found in the Yijing and the Huainanzi. The human participates in the same order as heaven and earth and completes it.</div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">天地人三才，缺一不可。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tiān dì rén sān cái, quē yī bù kě.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">Heaven, earth, and human , the three powers; none can be absent.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-violet">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">顶天立地</span>
          <span class="card-py">dǐng tiān lì dì</span>
          <span class="card-en">"head touching heaven, feet on earth" — a person of genuine integrity and moral stature</span>
        </div>
        <div class="card-def">顶 (to press upward against) + 天 + 立 (to stand upright) + 地. A person so morally substantial they fill the space between the two poles of the cosmos. Used for exceptional character.</div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">他是一个顶天立地的男子汉，从不逃避责任。</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tā shì yīgè dǐng tiān lì dì de nánzǐhàn, cóng bù táobì zérèn.</div>
            <div class="ex-en">He is a man of genuine integrity; he never evades responsibility.</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="card c-sienna">
        <div class="card-head">
          <span class="card-cn">天地良心</span>
          <span class="card-py">tiāndì liángxīn</span>
          <span class="card-en">"by heaven, earth, and conscience" — a strong oath of sincerity</span>
        </div>
        <div class="card-def">天地 as cosmic witnesses invoked alongside 良心 (conscience) in an oath of complete sincerity. Used when someone feels unjustly doubted.</div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example">
            <div class="ex-cn">天地良心，我真的没有说谎！</div>
            <div class="ex-py">Tiāndì liángxīn, wǒ zhēn de méiyǒu shuōhuǎng!</div>
            <div class="ex-en">By heaven, earth, and my conscience, I truly did not lie!</div>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div><!-- /cards -->

      <span class="sh-cn">成语</span>

      <span class="sh-en">Idioms — Creation &amp; Cosmos</span>
      <span class="sh-rule"></span>

    <div class="chengyu-grid">

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">开天辟地</span>
        <span class="cy-py">kāi tiān pì dì</span>
        <span class="cy-en">"to split open heaven and cleave earth" — to create the world; a groundbreaking, unprecedented achievement</span>
        <span class="cy-note">From the Pangu myth (盘古 Pángǔ). In the beginning, all was 混沌 hùndùn: undifferentiated chaos. Pangu broke it open. The light, clear elements rose and became Heaven; the heavy, turbid elements sank and became Earth. Pangu stood between them for 18,000 years until the separation was permanent. His body became the landscape: his breath the wind, his eyes the sun and moon, his flesh the soil. Used for any genuinely unprecedented, world-creating achievement. 这是一项开天辟地的发明。(This is an invention of world-creating magnitude.)</span>
      </div>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">天翻地覆</span>
        <span class="cy-py">tiān fān dì fù</span>
        <span class="cy-en">"heaven overturned, earth capsized" — a total upheaval; world-reversing change</span>
        <span class="cy-note">翻 fān (to overturn; to flip) + 覆 fù (to capsize). When the cosmos itself is described as flipped, the image captures change so total that the existing order has been inverted. Used for dramatic historical upheavals or situations where everything has changed beyond recognition. 经历了这场变革，国家发生了天翻地覆的变化。(After this transformation, the country underwent a total upheaval.)</span>
      </div>

      <div class="cy">
        <span class="cy-cn">惊天动地</span>
        <span class="cy-py">jīng tiān dòng dì</span>
        <span class="cy-en">"startling heaven, shaking earth" — earth-shaking; of tremendous power or significance</span>
        <span class="cy-note">惊 jīng (to startle; to alarm) + 动 dòng (to move; to shake). When something is so significant that heaven is startled and earth trembles, the hyperbole names genuine magnitude. Used for historic events, monumental achievements, and powerful works. 他们完成了一项惊天动地的壮举。(They accomplished an earth-shaking feat.)</span>
      </div>
