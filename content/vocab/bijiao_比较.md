      <span class="topic-hero-eyebrow">Vocab · Grammar · 词汇语法</span>
      <h1 class="topic-hero-title">比较</h1>
      <span class="topic-hero-title-py">bǐjiào</span>
      <p class="topic-hero-desc">To compare — and to qualify. 比较 functions as both a verb (comparing two things) and an adverb (softening a claim: "fairly," "relatively," "rather"). Mastering both uses unlocks a key dimension of natural Mandarin.</p>
    </header>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="etymology"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">字源</span><span class="sh-py">zìyuán</span><span class="sh-en">Etymology — 比 and 较 Unpacked</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="比">
      <div class="scholar-label">字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight</div>
      <p><strong>比 bǐ</strong> — two people standing side by side (the oracle bone form shows two matching human figures). The original meaning: to stand next to, to be aligned, to compare. Extended meanings: ratio (比例), for example (比如), score (比分). The side-by-side posture gives the character its core sense: comparison requires proximity.</p>
      <p><strong>较 jiào</strong> — 车 chē (chariot) + 交 jiāo (to cross; to intersect). Originally referred to the axle or hitch of a chariot — things that cross and connect. Extended to: to compare, to contend, to calculate. Together, 比较 = to stand beside and measure — comparison as both physical proximity and calculated difference.</p>
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="verb"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">动词用法</span><span class="sh-py">dòngcí yòngfǎ</span><span class="sh-en">比较 as Verb — Comparing Two Things</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">动词框架 · Verb Frame</span>
      <strong>比较 A 和 B</strong> → compare A and B<br>
      <strong>把 A 和 B 比较一下</strong> → compare A and B (with 把 for deliberate action)<br>
      <strong>拿 A 跟 B 比较</strong> → compare A to B (拿…跟 = take…and match against)
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-v">
        <div class="card-head"><span class="card-cn">比较 (verb)</span><span class="card-py">bǐjiào</span><span class="card-en">to compare; to draw a comparison</span></div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-v">V 动词 dòngcí</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">Used as a verb when you are explicitly comparing two things. Often followed by 一下 to soften it (let me compare for a moment), or by a result clause. Note: the verb 比较 is more formal — in casual speech 比 alone often handles simple comparison.</div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example"><div class="ex-cn">我们来比较一下这两个方案。</div><div class="ex-py">Wǒmen lái bǐjiào yīxià zhè liǎng gè fāng'àn.</div><div class="ex-en">Let's compare these two plans.</div></div>
          <div class="example"><div class="ex-cn">把这两种颜色比较一下，你更喜欢哪个？</div><div class="ex-py">Bǎ zhè liǎng zhǒng yánsè bǐjiào yīxià, nǐ gèng xǐhuān nǎ gè?</div><div class="ex-en">Compare these two colors — which do you prefer?</div></div>
        </div>
      </div>

    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="adverb"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">副词用法</span><span class="sh-py">fùcí yòngfǎ</span><span class="sh-en">比较 as Adverb — Relatively, Fairly, Rather</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

    <div class="scholar" data-glyph="比">
      <div class="scholar-label">辨析 biànxī · The Adverb Use</div>
      <p>When 比较 precedes an adjective or verb without a second term being compared, it functions as a degree adverb meaning "relatively," "fairly," or "rather." This is the more frequent everyday use. It hedges: instead of claiming something is simply 好 (good), saying 比较好 signals "good relative to expectations" or "good enough" — a characteristic Chinese tendency to qualify rather than assert.</p>
      <p>This hedging quality makes 比较 socially important. 这个比较难 (this is fairly difficult) is softer than 这个很难 (this is very difficult) — it leaves room for the listener to disagree or for the speaker to revise. Overusing absolutes (很/非常/极其) can sound blunt; 比较 provides a natural cushion.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="cards">

      <div class="card c-n">
        <div class="card-head"><span class="card-cn">比较 + adj</span><span class="card-py">bǐjiào + adj</span><span class="card-en">relatively / fairly / rather + adj</span></div>
        <div class="tags"><span class="tag tag-n">Pattern 句型</span></div>
        <div class="card-def">比较 + adjective = "fairly [adjective]." No comparison partner needed. 比较好 = fairly good / relatively good. 比较难 = rather difficult. 比较贵 = somewhat expensive. The hedging tone is always present — it implies "compared to some baseline" even when that baseline is unstated.</div>
        <div class="examples">
          <div class="example"><div class="ex-cn">这道题比较难，我做了很久。</div><div class="ex-py">Zhè dào tí bǐjiào nán, wǒ zuò le hěn jiǔ.</div><div class="ex-en">This problem is rather difficult — I worked on it for a long time.</div></div>
          <div class="example"><div class="ex-cn">今天天气比较好，适合出去走走。</div><div class="ex-py">Jīntiān tiānqì bǐjiào hǎo, shìhé chūqù zǒuzǒu.</div><div class="ex-en">The weather is fairly nice today — good for a walk.</div></div>
        </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="structures"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">比字句</span><span class="sh-py">bǐ zì jù</span><span class="sh-en">The 比 Comparison Frame — A 比 B + Adj</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">比较句框架 bǐjiào jù kuàngjià · Comparison Sentence Frame</span>
      <strong>A 比 B + adj</strong> → A is more [adj] than B<br>
      <strong>A 比 B + adj + 得多</strong> → A is much more [adj] than B<br>
      <strong>A 没有 B + adj</strong> → A is not as [adj] as B (negative comparison)<br>
      <strong>A 跟 B 一样 + adj</strong> → A is as [adj] as B (equality)<br><br>
      ⚠ Never use 更 gèng inside the 比 frame: 他比我<del>更</del>高 is wrong. Just: 他比我高.
    </div>

    <span class="section-anchor" id="scale"></span>
    <div class="section-head"><span class="sh-cn">程度梯度</span><span class="sh-py">chéngdù tīdù</span><span class="sh-en">Degree Scale — 比较 vs. 更 vs. 最</span><span class="sh-rule"></span></div>

    <div class="pattern">
      <span class="label">程度词梯 · Degree Word Ladder</span>
      <strong>比较 bǐjiào</strong> → relatively, fairly (moderate; hedging; no explicit comparison partner needed)<br>
      <strong>更 gèng</strong> → even more; more (explicit comparison implied; A is more than B)<br>
      <strong>最 zuì</strong> → most; the most (superlative; top of a set)<br><br>
      比较好 = fairly good · 更好 = even better (than X) · 最好 = the best
    </div>
