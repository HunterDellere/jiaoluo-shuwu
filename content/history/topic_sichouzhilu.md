      <p>This entry is in the authoring queue. "Silk Road" was coined by the German geographer Ferdinand von Richthofen in 1877; the Chinese term 丝绸之路 is a back-translation. There were many roads, not one, across many centuries: overland through the Hexi Corridor and the Tarim Basin, and maritime through Quanzhou and Guangzhou into Southeast Asia and beyond.</p>
      <p>Coming: Zhang Qian's missions under Han Wudi, the rise and fall of Tang dominance over the Tarim oases, the maritime routes that flourished as the land routes declined, and what travelled along them besides silk: Buddhism, paper, gunpowder, the Black Death.</p>
