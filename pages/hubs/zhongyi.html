The System

中医 · zhōng yī

中医 zhōngyī (中 = Chinese/central; 医 = medicine) is not a collection of folk remedies — it is a complete theoretical system developed over 2,500 years, with its own anatomy, physiology, pathology, diagnosis, and pharmacology. Its foundations are not empirical in the Western sense; they are cosmological: the human body is a microcosm of the universe, governed by the same principles of yin-yang, qi, and the five phases that govern everything else.

Where Western medicine asks "what is broken and how do we fix it?", Chinese medicine asks "where is the flow disrupted, and how do we restore it?" This path starts with the foundational theory and builds toward practice.

Reading Path

阴阳 · Foundations

阴阳 · Yin-Yang — Start here: the foundational polarity underlying all of Chinese cosmology, medicine, and philosophy. Nothing in 中医 makes sense without it.

中医 · Overview

中医 · Vocabulary Entry — The core vocabulary of the system: the key terms, their relationships, and the conceptual grammar that ties the whole framework together.

气 · Vital Force

气 · Qi — The vital force that circulates through the body along meridians. Understanding qi is prerequisite to understanding acupuncture, qigong, and diagnosis alike.

五行 · Five Phases

五行 · The Five Phases — Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water: the organizational framework that maps seasons, organs, emotions, flavours, and directions onto a single diagnostic grid.

阴阳五行 · Philosophy

阴阳五行 · Yin-Yang & Five Phases — The philosophical bedrock: how these two frameworks merged into the unified cosmological theory that underlies Chinese medicine, statecraft, and art.

针灸 · Acupuncture

针灸 · Acupuncture and Moxibustion — The two oldest physical interventions: needles that redirect qi through meridians, and the burning of mugwort to warm and tonify. Clinic, theory, and history together.

本草 · Herbology

本草 · Materia Medica — The pharmacopoeia of Chinese medicine: thousands of plant, mineral, and animal substances classified by flavour, temperature, and channel affinity. The Bencao Gangmu of Li Shizhen is the canonical text.

气功 · Cultivation

气功 · Qigong — Cultivating qi through coordinated movement, breath, and intention. Where acupuncture acts on the body from outside, qigong trains the practitioner to regulate their own flow from within.

中医 · Depth

中医 · Clinical and Historical Depth — The full scope: canonical texts, diagnostic methods (pulse, tongue, pattern differentiation), the history of the medicine from the Yellow Emperor's Classic to contemporary practice.

风水 · Cosmology Applied

风水 · Feng Shui — The same cosmological principles of qi, yin-yang, and the five phases applied to built space and landscape. A natural extension for readers who want to see how TCM theory radiates outward into Chinese civilisation at large.
