字源zìyuánEtymology — Territory in an Enclosure
字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight

The traditional form 圖 places content inside an enclosing border (囗 wéi): within that border sits 啚 bǐ, an old character for a granary or storehouse — a bounded place of stored resources. The whole character depicts a bounded representation of a piece of territory: a map, a plan, a schematic. The outer frame is the edge of the representational space; what's inside is the organized content.

The simplified form 图 keeps the enclosure but strips the interior to a simple cross shape — much more abstract, but the structural logic remains: something organized inside a defined boundary.

From "bounded visual representation" the meaning branches: a map is a plan of territory; a plan (deliberate intent) is a mental map of desired territory. This gives 图 its double life as both a noun (diagram, map) and a verb (to plan, to seek, to covet).

核心义héxīn yìCore Meanings — Map, Diagram, Plan, Seek
义场 yìchǎng · Meaning Field

Visual representation (noun). Any diagram, image, map, chart, or illustration: 地图 (map), 图片 (picture), 图表 (chart), 示意图 (schematic). In digital contexts: 图标 (icon), 图层 (layer in image editing). The sense of "bounded visual content" runs through all uses.

To plan / seek / intend (verb). 图谋 (to scheme), 企图 (to attempt, to harbor ambitions), 意图 (intention, intent). When 图 is used as a verb, it typically implies deliberate, calculated pursuit — often with a slightly ominous undertone. 图谋不轨 means to harbor treasonous plans.

To seek personal gain (贬义 negative register). 图利 (to seek profit/advantage), 图方便 (to seek convenience — doing something the easy way at someone else's expense). This usage often carries mild reproach.

核心词组héxīn cízǔKey Compounds
地图
dìtú
map
n
地 (earth, land) + 图 (diagram). The most literal use of 图 — a bounded visual representation of physical territory.
图书馆
túshūguǎn
library
n
图 (maps/drawings) + 书 (books) + 馆 (hall). Historically, 图书 referred to charts and texts together — the imperial archive of records and maps.
图片
túpiàn
picture · image · photo
n
The general term for any still image — photograph, illustration, or screenshot. Ubiquitous in digital contexts.
意图
yìtú
intention · intent
n
意 (meaning, will) + 图 (plan). 你的意图是什么？— What is your intention? Formal and slightly probing tone.
企图
qǐtú
to attempt · to try to · intent (often negative)
v/n
企 (to stand on tiptoe, to hope) + 图 (to plan). Implies ambition or attempted action — often with a suspicious or negative connotation.
图谋
túmóu
to scheme · to plot
v
图 (to plan) + 谋 (to scheme, plot). Negative — implies hidden, self-serving, or treacherous planning. 图谋不轨 = to harbor illegal/treasonous schemes.
试图
shìtú
to try to · to attempt
v
试 (to try, test) + 图 (to intend). Neutral — simply means to make an attempt. 他试图解决这个问题 — he tried to solve this problem.
数字时代shùzì shídàiDigital Age — 图 in the Visual Interface
图标
túbiāo
icon · pictogram
n
图 (image) + 标 (mark, label). The standard term for app icons and UI pictograms.
图层
túcéng
layer (in image/design software)
n
图 (image) + 层 (layer, floor). A Photoshop or Figma layer. Clean translation of "image layer."
图表
túbiǎo
chart · graph · diagram
n
图 (visual) + 表 (table, form, display). Any data visualization: bar charts, line graphs, org charts.
成语chéngyǔIdioms
鸿鹄之志
hóng hú zhī zhì
the ambition of a swan goose → grand aspirations; lofty goals
不 图 used here tangentially — the point is 志 (ambition), which pairs conceptually with 图 (to plan/seek). 燕雀安知鸿鹄之志哉 — how can a sparrow know the ambition of a great bird?
图穷匕见
tú qióng bǐ xiàn
when the map is unrolled, the dagger is revealed → a hidden scheme exposed; true intentions revealed
From the Warring States: an assassin hid a dagger inside a rolled map presented to the king. When the map was fully unrolled, the weapon appeared. Now means: the truth is out; the real plan is exposed.
