字源zìyuánEtymology — The Cliff-Shelter
字源洞见 zìyuán dòngjiàn · Etymological Insight

The oracle-bone and bronze-script form of 广 depicts a simple lean-to shelter: a slanted roof line hanging off a cliff face, open on the sides. A broad overhang, not a closed room. The core image is space that extends horizontally outward from a fixed point, sheltered but unenclosed. Wideness, not depth.

The traditional form 廣 elaborates this: inside the 广 shelter sits 黄 huáng (yellow/brightness) as a phonetic hint, plus additional strokes suggesting the interior expanse. The simplified character strips all of that back to three strokes, the original cliff-overhang in its purest form.

From "wide open shelter" the meaning generalized to width, broadness, and extension in all directions. In the modern era it extended further to broadcasting: 广播 (guǎngbō), literally "wide casting," the Chinese word for radio and television transmission.

部首作用bùshǒu zuòyòng广 as a Radical — Buildings and Open Spaces
部首家族 bùshǒu jiāzú · Radical Family

广 (radical 53) appears in characters that involve structures, rooms, and broad covered spaces:

店 diàn (shop, inn) , a place of shelter where goods are kept. 床 chuáng (bed) , a piece of furniture within a shelter. 厅 tīng (hall, living room) , an open interior space. 座 zuò (seat, measure word for buildings) , a seated position within a structure. 庙 miào (temple) , a sacred shelter. 府 fǔ (prefecture, official residence) , the broad hall of authority.

The semantic thread is consistent: where you see 广 at the top-left of a character, look for a built space or something that happens under shelter.

核心义héxīn yìCore Meanings — Wide, Broadcast, Expand
Structure · 结构 jiégòu
广 + noun → wide/extensive [noun]
广场 (wide open space), 广州 (broad region), 广告 (wide announcement = advertisement).
核心词组héxīn cízǔKey Compounds
广播
guǎngbō
radio broadcast · to broadcast
v/n
广 (wide) + 播 (to sow, scatter). The image of scattering signal broadly , the same metaphor as "broadcast" in English.
广场
guǎngchǎng
public square · plaza
n
广 (wide) + 场 (open space). 天安门广场 is the archetypal 广场. Any open civic gathering place.
广告
guǎnggào
advertisement · ad
n
广 (wide) + 告 (to announce). "Wide announcement." The Chinese word for advertising captures the medium better than the English one.
广东
Guǎngdōng
Guangdong Province
proper n
The "broad east." Paired with 广西 (broad west). Together they defined the southern administrative region of the empire.
广州
Guǎngzhōu
Guangzhou (Canton)
proper n
Capital of Guangdong. 州 is an administrative region. The historical gateway for maritime trade with the West.
广泛
guǎngfàn
extensive · widespread · broad
adj
广 (wide) + 泛 (to float, spread broadly). Describes scope: 广泛的影响 (widespread influence), 广泛的兴趣 (wide-ranging interests).
宽广
kuānguǎng
broad · vast · expansive
adj
宽 (width from side to side) + 广 (broad expanse). Describes physical space or a generous character: 胸怀宽广 (broad-minded, generous of heart).
推广
tuīguǎng
to promote · to spread · to popularize
v
推 (to push) + 广 (wide). To push something out broadly , used for promoting products, policies, or languages.
成语chéngyǔIdioms
大庭广众
dà tíng guǎng zhòng
a large hall and a wide crowd → in public; before everyone
在大庭广众之下 , in front of everyone. Used to emphasize the public, exposed nature of an action.
地大物博
dì dà wù bó
vast land and abundant resources
Standard phrase describing China's geographic richness. 博 (abundant, learned) pairs with 大 (vast). Often heard in patriotic contexts.
