# HSK 3.0 Reference — Field Notes on Chinese

Complete vocabulary, character, and grammar reference for all 9 levels of the New HSK (《国际中文教育中文水平等级标准》GF 0025—2021, Ministry of Education PRC, published March 2021, effective July 2021).

## Sources

- ivankra/hsk30 — cleaned HSK 3.0 vocabulary with pinyin, POS, and traditional forms (github.com/ivankra/hsk30)
- krmanik/HSK-3.0 — HSK character lists and grammar point reference (github.com/krmanik/HSK-3.0)
- Official standard: 《国际中文教育中文水平等级标准》GF 0025—2021 (chinesetest.cn)

Fetched: April 2026.

## Coverage Notes

Levels 7–9 are combined into a single advanced band per the official standard; all three sub-levels are covered in hsk-7-9.md.

Traditional forms are collapsed when identical to simplified. When they differ, entries appear as `simplified / traditional`.

Vocabulary counts reflect words introduced at each level. The ivankra/hsk30 dataset does not repeat words across levels. Grammar point counts reflect distinct topic headings from the official standard as organized in the krmanik/HSK-3.0 grammar files.

POS abbreviations used: V (verb), N (noun), Adj (adjective), Adv (adverb), Num (numeral), MW or M (measure word), Aux (auxiliary/modal verb), Prep (preposition), Conj (conjunction), Pron (pronoun), Part (particle).

English glosses are drawn from Mandarin Chinese knowledge. Items marked `[gloss needed]` are lower-frequency entries (primarily in HSK 7–9) that require specialist verification. HSK 1–4 entries are fully glossed; HSK 5–6 have partial coverage for less common compound forms.
